# Deal marketplaces for first customers

Status: **Research**

Research date: 2026-07-20

## Verdict

Deal marketplaces can plausibly produce Tecotype's first few customers, but no
route currently passes the [acquisition gate](./acquisition/readme.md). The
best candidates are **Secret**, **Dealify**, **BitsDuJour**, and, conditionally,
**AppSumo**. Each remains **Parked** until the platform supplies exact
comparable funnel data, accepts Tecotype's recurring offer, and confirms
commercial terms.

The marketplace tradeoff is consistent:

- recurring-friendly marketplaces preserve Tecotype's product and price but
  publish little exact evidence that they will expose an unknown product;
- platforms that promise product-specific email, affiliate, advertising, and
  editorial promotion usually expect a lifetime licence, a deep discount,
  platform checkout, or a large revenue share.

The next action is not to publish the same deal everywhere. Ask the first four
platforms for one comparable data packet, then advance only a route whose exact
surface can support 100 attributable first-time paid customers within the USD
61 incremental-CAC ceiling.

## What qualifies for this review

The user must be able to discover Tecotype on a platform-controlled page,
email, recommendation, or publisher placement without already knowing
Tecotype. The platform must do more than accept a directory entry.

The desired path is:

```text
zero-history person encounters a Tecotype deal on the host
→ follows an attributable partner link
→ purchases the standard direct desktop subscription
→ installs the standard macOS, Windows, or Linux build
→ connects a real mailbox
→ remains paid
```

The platform may receive purchase and aggregate attribution data. Mailbox
credentials, addresses, domains, messages, queries, and derived mailbox data
must never enter the marketplace.

Research assumes a launch-ready direct desktop product for channel planning.
Website billing, licensing, and the public acquisition experience are not
shipped today. A marketplace offer is not approved merely because it appears
below.

## Economics visible before outreach

These are channel fees only. They do not establish that the customer is
qualified, retained, or incremental.

The examples use the already documented €99 first-year affiliate offer solely
to expose fee pressure. That price is not automatically approved for a deal
marketplace. At the latest available [ECB reference rate on 2026-07-17](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/stats/policy_and_exchange_rates/euro_reference_exchange_rates/html/index.en.html),
EUR 1 equalled USD 1.1435.

| Platform | Public fee applied to €99 first-year revenue | Result before payment, attribution, administration, or support |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| BitsDuJour | 50% = €49.50, about USD 56.62 | Only about USD 4.38 remains inside the USD 61 CAC ceiling. The offer would also fail BitsDuJour's 40%-off minimum because €99 is only 33.6% below €149. |
| PitchGround Featured | 60% = €59.40, about USD 67.92 | Fails the CAC ceiling before other cost. |
| PitchGround Marketplace | 30% = €29.70, about USD 33.97 | Fee can fit, but PitchGround does not run a product-specific campaign for a Marketplace listing. |
| DealFuel | 25% = €24.75, about USD 28.31 | Fee can fit; capacity, paid bridge, product checkout, and required discount remain unresolved. |
| Proven performance plan | 30% = €29.70, about USD 33.97 | Fee can fit before its undisclosed promotional fee; current eligibility does not. |
| Secret, Dealify, AppSumo, NachoNacho, StackCommerce | Not public | Require a written quote and complete cost schedule. |

BitsDuJour's published minimum discount would put a €149 annual product at no
more than €89.40 before its commission. This is below the existing €99
first-year affiliate experiment and should not be offered without new pricing
and retention evidence.

## The exact data request

Send the same request to Secret, Dealify, BitsDuJour, and AppSumo so their
answers are comparable:

1. For each named, recent, unknown-brand productivity product and each available
   full email-client campaign, provide unique listing views, product-specific
   email recipients and opens, clicks, checkout starts, first payments,
   refunds, and 90-day or first-renewal retained customers.
2. Separate existing users from people first introduced by the platform.
3. State the dates and placements that produced the results. Do not combine
   homepage, newsletter, affiliate, paid-ad, search, and repeat-user traffic.
4. Quote every cost: revenue share, setup, placement, affiliate, payment,
   refund, payout, currency, minimum discount, exclusivity, and support fees.
5. Confirm whether the platform accepts:
   - a recurring desktop subscription, never lifetime access;
   - one standard direct build and licence, with no marketplace-specific binary;
   - an explicit partner link or checkout code rather than advertising pixels;
   - ordinary renewal at €149/year after the disclosed first period;
   - aggregate paid, refund, and retention reporting without mailbox data.
6. Confirm the expected campaign deliverables and whether any exposure is
   guaranteed: exact page, email, social, affiliate, editorial, paid-media,
   duration, and geographic eligibility.

Advance only if the exact data supports 100 attributable first-time paid
customers over an evidenced useful life and complete incremental CAC can stay
at or below USD 61.

## Recommended contact sequence

1. **Secret:** request the 95-redemption Superhuman cohort's elapsed time,
   activation-to-paid conversion, refund/cancellation, retention, exact page
   views, promotional placements, and vendor pricing.
2. **Dealify:** request the same funnel for SenderStack, Vocal, and two
   unknown-brand annual productivity deals; require a direct checkout or
   standard-product path.
3. **BitsDuJour:** request promotion-level results for Mailbird, eM Client, and
   The Bat!, plus a recurring-subscription ruling and a privacy-safe direct
   checkout attribution method.
4. **AppSumo:** ask specifically for an annual campaign that renews directly at
   the ordinary price, exact Mailbird performance, negotiated revenue share,
   and product-specific marketing deliverables.
5. Approach **NachoNacho** after initial paid use can establish product
   maturity. Approach **Proven** only after there are enough business customers
   to evaluate portfolio overlap.

Do not apply to all platforms at once. The same bargain offer appearing across
several marketplaces weakens exclusivity, confounds attribution, and can
attract support-heavy customers who do not represent the recurring
professional-email market.
