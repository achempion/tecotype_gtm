## Major redirects

1. Sealed discovery converged on inspectability after mail becomes a file, so
   coordinator research redirected from more archive anecdotes to the
   free-to-paid bridge, OS/file discovery, and adequate free viewers.
2. Provider guides showed real comparable recommendations, so the provider seed
   was separated into provider-controlled distribution versus Tecotype-owned
   acquisition instead of treating compatibility as partnership evidence.
3. Account-authentication evidence was not merged: passkeys, OAuth redirect
   regressions, tenant policy, and protocol absence are separate failure
   mechanisms.
4. Workflow evidence was redirected from generic productivity toward
   reply-aware state, recipient value, and current paid-client behavior.
5. The leading expectation shifted toward a null opportunity result: a pain may
   be confirmed while recommendation or same-person paid causality remains
   unproven.
6. A 2026-07-19 current-source recheck found that Microsoft now documents PST
   search in new Outlook for supported Windows configurations. The Outlook
   finalist was narrowed to locally present Classic Outlook PST/OST content
   omitted after official scope/index checks; generic PST search was removed
   from the promise.

## Stopping rounds

Both rounds began after the 60-minute floor at
`2026-07-19T14:22:56+0200`.

1. **Role and purchase-behavior round.** Queries tested accountants, lawyers,
   self-employed users, and explicit Outlook-search-to-eM-Client switching.
   Results repeated already classified Outlook search, multi-account, and
   broader New Outlook complaints. The accounting surface lacked the exact
   confirmed mechanism and paid behavior; no legal surface closed the gap.
   No new pain family emerged and no same-person payment blocker was removed.
2. **Current fix, platform, and partner-surface round.** Queries tested July
   2026 Microsoft fixes, current paid switching, provider-controlled client
   recommendations, and Microsoft Store PST/OST search. The opened July 2026
   Classic Outlook known-issues page did not list a fix for the retained
   body-index omission, while the current troubleshooting page preserved the
   many configuration and indexing alternatives already used to narrow the
   segment. Google Workspace continued to recommend Outlook and Apple Mail,
   not Tecotype. No provider commitment, qualifying distribution surface, new
   candidate, or blocker removal appeared.

Research stopped at `2026-07-19T14:23:38+0200` because every retained route
had been classified and independently audited, all time and coverage floors
had been exceeded, and these two materially different rounds produced neither
a new candidate nor a blocker removal.
