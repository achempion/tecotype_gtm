# Marketing management

Status: Working operating guide

Last reviewed: 2026-07-22

## Purpose

This document describes how marketing work should be understood, organized,
and managed for Tecotype. It is an operating guide, not a campaign plan or an
approved organization design.

Marketing connects a market, a product promise, distribution, customer
behavior, and revenue. It is not a downstream service that begins after the
product has been built.

The manager's central question is:

> Which customer behavior are we trying to change, what currently prevents it,
> and which coordinated intervention could change it?

## The marketing system

A healthy marketing organization repeatedly runs this loop:

```text
Choose a market
→ understand the customer
→ define positioning and the offer
→ create messages and evidence
→ distribute them
→ convert interest into product use or a sales conversation
→ activate and retain customers
→ measure behavior
→ revise the assumptions
```

Examples of customer behavior include:

- Recognize a problem.
- Remember Tecotype when considering an email client.
- Visit a relevant page.
- Download the application.
- Connect a mail account.
- Complete a meaningful workflow.
- Start a paid subscription.
- Continue paying.
- Recommend Tecotype to another person.

Marketing execution should be organized around these behaviors, not around the
volume of assets, posts, campaigns, or experiments produced.

## Routes to market

### Self-serve or product-led

The customer discovers, evaluates, and buys the product without speaking to
sales.

```text
Audience → website → download → activation → subscription → retention
```

Typical capabilities include:

- Product marketing and positioning.
- Brand, copy, design, and product demonstrations.
- Organic and paid acquisition.
- Landing pages and conversion.
- Onboarding and activation.
- Lifecycle communication.
- Funnel experimentation.

This is the intended initial motion for Tecotype Individual. It remains an
operating hypothesis until the audience, acquisition route, activation path,
and willingness to pay are validated.

### Sales-assisted or enterprise

Marketing develops demand and sales manages a longer buying process.

```text
Target account → engagement → sales meeting → opportunity → contract
→ deployment → renewal → expansion
```

Typical capabilities include demand generation, account-based marketing,
field marketing, sales enablement, revenue operations, enterprise onboarding,
and customer success.

This is not Tecotype Individual's current motion. Shared inboxes, support
automation, enterprise administration, and a managed sales motion would require
separate validation, economics, product decisions, and privacy boundaries.

Do not combine self-serve and enterprise funnels in one report. Their buyers,
timelines, economics, handoffs, and useful metrics differ.

## Role contracts

The same person may cover several contracts at an early-stage company. The
contracts should still remain distinct so work, dependencies, and outcomes do
not become ambiguous.

| Role or capability | Primary ownership | What the manager must know | Useful success evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Marketing leadership | Market strategy, budget, organization, and portfolio of bets | Connect business goals to market choices and balance short- and long-term work | Qualified customer and revenue growth, efficient learning, stronger market position |
| Customer and market research | Customer situations, problems, alternatives, segments, and willingness to pay | Research exists to change consequential decisions, not to accumulate interviews | Assumptions invalidated, segments clarified, product or market decisions changed |
| Product marketing | Audience, positioning, messaging, launches, competitive intelligence, and customer proof | Must influence product and offer decisions, not merely describe completed work | Message comprehension, qualified demand, activation, adoption, win evidence |
| Brand strategy | What the product should mean and how it becomes memorable | Brand is accumulated memory and preference, not a visual-style project | Awareness, consideration, branded demand, direct traffic, consistent perception |
| Creative direction | Coherence and quality across copy, design, demonstration, and video | Creative needs a clear brief and a small, qualified decision group | Clear, distinctive work that performs appropriately for its purpose |
| Copywriting | Headlines, pages, advertisements, emails, and campaign narratives | Good copy depends on positioning, observed customer language, proof, and context | Comprehension, qualified response, conversion, qualitative resonance |
| Design | Visual communication across the site, campaigns, demonstrations, and growth surfaces | Brand design, performance creative, and product design optimize for different outcomes | Clarity, distinctiveness, production quality, conversion where appropriate |
| Creative operations or producer | Timelines, budgets, agencies, shoots, and asset delivery | This role removes coordination load but does not decide market strategy | Predictable delivery, controlled cost, fewer blocked launches |
| Content marketing | Useful material that earns attention, demonstrates expertise, and builds trust | Every piece needs an audience, distribution plan, and commercial role | Qualified discovery, assisted conversion, subscriptions, use in customer decisions |
| Social media | Channel-native publishing, participation, and community conversation | Each platform has different behavior; publishing volume is not a strategy | Relevant reach, engagement quality, community response, qualified traffic |
| Performance marketing | Paid search, social, display, video, and acquisition economics | Requires a credible offer, sufficient creative variants, and reliable conversion data | Incremental paid customers, CAC, payback, LTV/CAC, learning velocity |
| Demand generation | Integrated B2B programs that create and accelerate sales pipeline | Requires clear personas, buying stages, account priorities, and sales alignment | Qualified pipeline, opportunities, velocity, conversion, revenue |
| Lifecycle or CRM | Activation, engagement, retention, renewal, and expansion communication | Needs trustworthy product events and lifecycle states; cannot repair weak product value | Activation, retained use, subscription retention, expansion, reduced churn |
| Marketing operations | Audience data, campaign systems, automation, tracking, and governance | This is infrastructure that enables marketing; it does not create demand by itself | Reliable data, correct campaigns, faster delivery, clean handoffs |
| Revenue operations | Shared systems and definitions across marketing, sales, and customer success | Resolves routing, ownership, lifecycle definitions, and reporting disputes | Reliable pipeline data, fast routing, accurate forecasting, fewer handoff failures |
| Account-based marketing | Personalized programs for a bounded set of high-value accounts | Appropriate only when contract value justifies expensive personalization | Account engagement, meetings, opportunity acceleration, account revenue |
| Partner marketing | Co-marketing, integrations, partner enablement, and referred demand | A relationship or announcement is not automatically a distribution channel | Partner-sourced qualified customers, pipeline, or activated integrations |
| Growth product management | Signup, activation, conversion, monetization, and product growth loops | Bridges product and marketing and requires engineering, design, and analytics capacity | Funnel improvement, activation, conversion, retention, valid learning |
| Growth design and engineering | Landing pages, onboarding, experiments, and reusable growth systems | Needs a prioritized experiment portfolio, not an endless ticket queue | Valid experiments, durable learning, measurable funnel improvement |
| Marketing analytics | Measurement, segmentation, causal analysis, and forecasting | Attribution is an estimate; correlation and incrementality are different claims | More reliable decisions, trustworthy metrics, improved experimental quality |

## Cross-functional execution

Most marketing failures occur between roles rather than inside one specialty.

A typical self-serve workflow is:

1. Research identifies an audience, situation, and problem.
2. Product marketing defines the positioning, promise, proof, and competitive
   frame.
3. Creative turns the strategy into pages, demonstrations, advertisements, and
   content.
4. Acquisition places that evidence in front of relevant people.
5. Product, design, and engineering improve download, setup, and activation.
6. Lifecycle communication helps users reach and repeat meaningful value.
7. Analytics identifies where the funnel breaks and which explanation remains
   plausible.
8. The team changes the underlying hypothesis or execution.

Important interface failures include:

- Acquisition starts before the audience, offer, or message is credible.
- Creative receives a list of deliverables instead of a strategic brief.
- Product marketing is asked to announce work but cannot influence the product
  or offer.
- Lifecycle lacks reliable product events and sends generic communication.
- Sales and marketing use different definitions of a qualified opportunity.
- Analytics reports activity without showing progression through the customer
  journey.
- Local channel metrics improve while customer quality or retention declines.

The manager owns the quality of these interfaces even when specialists own the
individual work.

## The required brief

Before approving a campaign, launch, or experiment, require a short brief with:

- Business objective.
- Specific audience.
- Observed customer situation or problem.
- Desired customer behavior.
- Promise being made.
- Evidence supporting the promise.
- Funnel stage.
- Channel and reason for choosing it.
- Offer and next action.
- Baseline behavior.
- Primary metric and guardrails.
- Expected economics when spend is involved.
- Assets and dependencies.
- Responsible owner.
- Learning objective.
- Decision that will follow the result.

An outcome is not automatically a learning objective.

- Outcome: increase downloads.
- Learning objective: determine whether on-device Better search produces more
  qualified download intent than keyboard speed for a defined audience.

## The metric tree

Use one shared customer and economic model rather than assigning disconnected
metrics to departments.

An initial Tecotype Individual metric tree is:

```text
Qualified awareness
→ relevant website visit
→ download
→ successful installation
→ account connection
→ first meaningful mail workflow
→ evaluation engagement
→ paid subscription
→ retained subscription
```

The exact definition of the first meaningful workflow must be validated. It
should represent experienced value, not merely completion of setup.

For every useful stage, track:

- Volume.
- Conversion from the previous stage.
- Time to progress.
- Cost where applicable.
- Acquisition source.
- Audience and platform segment.
- Retention or later customer quality.

### Three levels of marketing metrics

1. Business outcomes: customers, revenue, retention, and payback.
2. Customer behavior: qualified visits, downloads, activation, adoption, and
   buying conversations.
3. Operational signals: impressions, clicks, opens, asset delivery, and event
   attendance.

Operational signals help diagnose work but are not success by themselves.
Cheap clicks, high engagement, and large lead counts can all coexist with poor
customer quality.

Brand work needs longer measurement windows and should not be evaluated only
through last-click attribution. Performance work should be evaluated through
incremental customers and economics, not solely through conversions reported
by an advertising platform.

## Management cadence

### Weekly funnel and learning review

Review:

- Material changes in the shared funnel.
- Differences by audience, source, and platform.
- Experiment results and confidence.
- Current constraints.
- Decisions, owners, and follow-up dates.

Do not turn this into a recital of completed activity.

### Weekly execution review

For each active launch or campaign, review:

- Objective and responsible owner.
- Current state.
- Dependencies and handoffs.
- Decisions required.
- Risks to timing, truth, or quality.

### Creative review

Keep creative review separate from the funnel meeting. Evaluate whether the
work communicates the intended promise clearly, credibly, and distinctly.
Avoid consensus editing and give final creative authority to a small number of
qualified people.

### Monthly portfolio review

Decide whether to continue, scale, modify, or stop each material marketing bet.
Reallocate budget and capacity using evidence, strategic importance, and the
remaining uncertainty.

### Quarterly strategy review

Revisit:

- Audience and segment assumptions.
- Positioning and competitive changes.
- Route to market.
- The primary funnel constraint.
- Budget allocation.
- Missing organizational capabilities.

Do not change positioning every time one execution performs poorly.

## Manager questions

For any proposed activity, ask:

- Which audience is this for?
- What is observed, and what is assumed?
- Which customer behavior should change?
- Why is that behavior not happening now?
- What promise are we making?
- What makes the promise credible?
- Why is this the appropriate channel?
- Where does the customer go next?
- Who owns that handoff?
- What is the baseline?
- How will success be distinguished from noise?
- What decision will follow the result?

For a proposed hire, ask:

- Which recurring constraint requires this capability?
- Does the role own strategy, execution, infrastructure, or a channel?
- Which inputs must exist before the person can succeed?
- Which other roles must work with this person?
- Which outcome can the role influence but not independently control?
- Is the need permanent, or could a specialist or agency handle a bounded
  project?

## Early hiring order

At Tecotype's current stage, capabilities matter more than a large role list.
A reasonable sequence is:

1. Founder-led customer research and market selection.
2. Product marketing: audience, positioning, offer, proof, pricing, and launch.
3. Strong copy and design that demonstrate the product.
4. A small number of attributable acquisition experiments.
5. Reliable download-to-activation measurement.
6. Lifecycle communication around evaluation, purchase, and retention.
7. Dedicated channel specialists only after repeatable demand or a recurring
   operational constraint is visible.

A dedicated performance marketer is premature until the offer, website,
activation path, creative throughput, and measurement are sufficiently
reliable. Enterprise demand generation, account-based marketing, and heavy
revenue operations are premature unless Tecotype chooses a sales-assisted
motion.

Do not hire one generic marketing person and implicitly assign research,
positioning, copy, design, acquisition, analytics, operations, and lifecycle to
them. If one person covers several capabilities, state the priority order and
which responsibilities remain intentionally unstaffed.

## Common management errors

- Treating marketing as a ticket queue for pages and announcements.
- Selecting channels before selecting the audience and promise.
- Expecting deterministic forecasts from a probabilistic market.
- Building elaborate automation and attribution before validating the message
  and offer.
- Requiring every activity to produce immediate, individually attributable
  revenue.
- Optimizing clicks, leads, or signups without examining activation and
  retention.
- Giving every stakeholder equal authority over copy and creative.
- Confusing more experiments with faster learning.
- Combining self-serve and enterprise metrics.
- Copying the marketing organization of a company at a different scale.

## Tecotype boundaries

### Product truth

Marketing must follow the current claims boundary in
[positioning.md](./positioning.md) and verify product and platform status
against `../tecotype` before publication. Planned capabilities must not be
presented as shipped.

The current public boundary remains Mac-first. Windows and Linux support may be
planned or assumed for future distribution research, but availability cannot be
claimed before the relevant releases ship.

### Voice and emotional contract

Marketing should lead with calm control and use keyboard speed, local
operation, Better search, and precise organization as concrete proof. It should
not copy the urgency, gamification, or performance theatre of louder
competitors.

See [positioning.md](./positioning.md) for the complete hierarchy and voice
rules.

### Acquisition and distribution

Acquisition is any measurable route that brings a prospective customer into
the evaluation funnel.

In this workspace, distribution has a narrower definition. Count a partner as
distribution only when it can introduce Tecotype to people with zero existing
users through permanent pre-install discovery and an attributable
first-action-to-paid funnel. Listings, mentions, launch coverage, referrals,
and campaigns remain acquisition unless they meet that complete definition.

See [next-acquisition-funnel.md](./next-acquisition-funnel.md) for the current
decision and [distribution/partnerships.md](./distribution/partnerships.md) for
the partnership boundary.
