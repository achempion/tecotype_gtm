# Castellum search traffic

Research date: **2026-07-17**

Market: **Google United States, English, desktop Windows for live checks**

Target: **`castellummail.com`, including subdomains only where explicitly
stated**

DataForSEO Labs is a modeled third-party snapshot. Live SERPs show visible
results at one time and place. Neither source exposes Castellum’s visits,
clicks, installs, account connections, or retained use.

## Headline result

Castellum has one weak modeled ranking and no qualified live visibility in the
queries tested.

- Domain Rank Overview returned one organic keyword.
- Ranked Keywords returned the same one row.
- Relevant Pages returned only the homepage.
- Modeled organic ETV was **0.672**.
- Modeled paid keyword count was zero.
- The one keyword, `castellum ai`, is heavily ambiguous with an unrelated
  compliance company at `castellum.ai`.
- Same-day live checks did not surface the email product for that query, two
  brand/descriptive-brand queries, or three category queries.

This is consistent with a site and product that first became public 18 days
before capture. It is not evidence that the homepage has never received a
search visit.

The exact-brand problem is material. “Castellum” already refers to:

- a Swedish property company;
- a US defense/technology company;
- an AML/KYC AI company;
- the Latin word and related reference pages.

Adding `mail` or `email client` did not make the new product visible in the
tested top-ten result sets. Brand memory alone therefore does not yet produce
reliable search navigation.

## Category interpretation

The tested category queries came directly from the landing page:

- private AI email client for Mac;
- keyboard-first Mac email client;
- email client for managers.

The domain’s absence rejects current top-ten visibility for those strings. It
does not establish that:

- the terms are the best demand targets;
- no long-tail variant ranks;
- no person has searched the brand;
- no result appears in another country, language, device, or personalized
  context;
- future authority cannot change the result.

The visible competitors and roundups also show that broad private-AI and Mac
email terms are contested by established products and publishers. A new
single-page domain will not earn qualified distribution merely by repeating
those words.

## Search readiness

Observed strengths:

- title, description, hero, and feature copy use a consistent Mac/private-AI
  category;
- the manager audience is named explicitly;
- Open Graph and Twitter card metadata exist;
- the page contains crawlable product detail and one product screenshot;
- GitHub release pages provide separate crawlable version history.

Observed limitations:

- no canonical link;
- `robots.txt` and `sitemap.xml` return GitHub Pages 404 responses;
- no structured data or `hreflang`;
- only one product page;
- no provider, use-case, comparison, help, or pricing-page program;
- new legal pages were added only on the capture date;
- the brand collides with several larger entities;
- the broad privacy statement needs qualification;
- the public Gmail CTA is ahead of the latest explicit authorization status.

Technical hygiene alone would not solve the name collision or produce
qualified demand. More pages would also create risk if they repeat
unverified provider, privacy, or feature claims.

## Paid-search boundary

- The Google advertiser lookup for `castellum mail` returned `No Search

- The Google creative lookup for `castellummail.com` across the requested

These are narrow Google archive snapshots. They do not rule out another
identity, network, market, date range, sponsorship, or unindexed campaign.

## Verdict

Current organic search is not a repeatable Castellum acquisition path.

The one modeled rank is:

- low;
- estimated rather than observed analytics;
- attached to an ambiguous query;
- not reproduced in the live result set;
- worth less than one modeled visit.

The manager-specific page is better suited to direct message testing than
broad SEO at this stage. For Tecotype, the useful lesson is to validate one
accurate job and activation path before expanding category content. Ranking
or modeled ETV without account connection, first value, and return is not
acquisition success.
