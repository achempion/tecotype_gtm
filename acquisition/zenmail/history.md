## What changed or drifted

### Implementation story

Earlier-dated current articles say:

- Rust;
- Tauri;
- WKWebView;
- under-15-MB binary;
- target cold start under 100 ms;
- encrypted SQLite.

The current homepage metadata/structured data says:

- SwiftUI plus AppKit;
- native Swift mail engine;
- WebKit only for sanitized email bodies;
- approximately 150 MB of memory.

This may indicate a substantial rewrite, templated/stale editorial content, or
another explanation not visible publicly. It should be reported as a conflict,
not as a known migration. The binary-size and memory figures are different
measurements and are not themselves treated as contradictory.

### Privacy wording

The current homepage says “protected local database” and explains macOS
account-level file restriction. The current privacy policy, About page, and
several articles still say encrypted database. The site does not explain the
wording change.

### Offer

All current public paths lead to the private-beta waitlist. The FAQ frames the
beta as free and the eventual product as paid. Terms permit features and
pricing to change and mention Polar, but there is no active public purchase
path.

### Editorial direction

The dated sequence moves from product/engineering and inbox philosophy into:

1. Gmail problem-solving content;
2. category and competitor comparisons;
3. trust/security proof;
4. adjacent Mac-app discovery;
5. timely migration demand around Notion Mail.

That pattern is consistent with an SEO acquisition program. Its effectiveness
is unobservable, and four representative pages failed to capture their exact
checked top-10 queries.

## Older ZenMail identity

Search results expose a 2020 “ZenMail: Hey for Gmail” browser extension with a
Screener-like concept and different domain/contact identity. Similar product
ideas are not enough to establish continuity. No public operator name, legal
entity, redirect, announcement, or archival link connected it to the current
Mac app in this pass.

Accordingly, the old extension is an identity caveat, not a previous stage of
this timeline.
