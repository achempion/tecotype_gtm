# Wave 3 acquisition synthesis

Research date: **2026-07-17**

Archive notice: this is competitor evidence, not a current recommendation.
The [current decision](../next-acquisition-funnel.md) and
[acquisition gate](./readme.md#one-hard-gate) supersede every sequence below.

Scope: **seven emerging, changing, or discontinued email-client acquisition
models**

This synthesis compares:

- [ReplylessAI](./replyless-ai/readme.md)
- [Orchid / Zero](./orchid/readme.md)
- [MonoMail](./monomail/readme.md)
- [Extra](./extra/readme.md)
- [Airo Mail](./airo-mail/readme.md)
- [Notion Mail](./notion-mail/readme.md)
- [Newton Mail](./newton-mail/readme.md)

Every metric is a point-in-time public observation, provider estimate,
first-party statement, store or repository counter, or individual anecdote.
No client exposed a complete source-to-retained-customer attribution model.

This wave assumes the Tecotype client works and product delivery is handled
elsewhere. The recommendations are limited to positioning, public proof,
acquisition, conversion communication, lifecycle marketing, channel design,
trust, and attribution.

No competitor client, installer, application, package, binary, release asset,
source checkout, or update payload was downloaded, opened, mounted, installed,
executed, or inspected. No account, mailbox, trial, OAuth flow, form, SMS,
checkout, subscription, or payment flow was used.

## Executive answer

Wave 3 supports one marketing discipline:

> Build acquisition around a specific promise, permanent public proof, source
> identity, and a current commercial state; require paid evidence before
> calling the result distribution.

Each client demonstrates one useful part:

- **ReplylessAI** has the wave's clearest current paid signal. Three Product
  Hunt launches, directories, weekly updates, and plain lifecycle email are
  visible alongside a small Stripe-verified paid base. Only one update email
  is tied to a purchase, and the customer's original acquisition source is
  unknown.
- **Orchid's Zero era** shows how open source, Product Hunt, Y Combinator, and
  community launch surfaces can leave a large durable public footprint.
  Changing from an email client into a broader assistant split the category,
  domain, CTA, and commercial path.
- **MonoMail** drew its strongest visible current engagement from one relevant
  community post while its new owned domain had no modeled search terms. Its
  stars, releases, and cumulative asset-download events still stop before
  unique installs, activation, retention, or payment.
- **Extra** has a memorable action-first “life inbox,” tracked editorial and
  creator routes, stores, launch coverage, and recent paid creatives. It is
  free, has no public economic path, and its open-access homepage conflicts
  with stale invite-only store copy.
- **Airo Mail** exposed two materially different public homepage states: a
  direct mobile-consumer offer and a captured/indexed solo-founder offer. Both
  make workflows concrete and offer a no-card trial; only the latter exposed
  the strongest evaluation element, a no-sign-in demo. The inconsistency can
  create conversion risk, and its tiny discovery footprint means neither the
  $8 Personal nor $300 Pro annual commitment validates price.
- **Notion Mail** combined an existing brand, waitlist, press, free Gmail
  offer, store presence, and community attention. The inbox is now scheduled
  to close on September 22, 2026, and no public source shows an attributable
  paid inbox cohort.
- **Newton Mail** retains visible brand affection alongside trust debt.
  Repeated shutdowns now coexist with persistent competitor-owned alternative
  pages; its 2026 relaunch remains brand-led, while current homepage, access,
  platform, store, and changelog states conflict.

For Tecotype, the strongest lead remains:

> **Find the email you only half remember—even when you remember it in another
> language.**

The Copenhagen-ticket result makes that promise inspectable. Calm, privacy,
keyboard control, and multiple accounts should reinforce the result rather
than compete with it in the headline.

## What appears to support marketing and distribution

### 1. A precise reason to remember the product

The strongest messages in the wave let a reader repeat the idea:

- Airo exposed two repeatable promises: email that organizes itself for mobile
  consumers, and superpowered email for solo founders.
- Extra: a life inbox organized around what needs attention today.
- Notion Mail: an inbox that thinks like you through custom views.
- Newton's relaunch: the calm inbox returns.
- MonoMail: a visually minimal, open-source Android client.

ReplylessAI's broad AI-client offer is less distinctive, but its founder story
and transparent progress create specificity around the business.

Tecotype should not combine every benefit into one launch claim. The
cross-language retrieval result is the most concrete current candidate:

```text
“train ticket to copenhagen”
→ “Din togbillet til København”
```

Use one secondary proof sequence for keyboard triage. Keep “AI email,”
“productivity,” and “smart inbox” out of the lead unless a buyer can verify the
mechanism immediately.

### 2. Evaluation before authorization

One captured/indexed Airo variant's no-sign-in demonstration is the most
transferable funnel design in the wave. The direct page did not expose it, so
Airo does not prove that the pattern is universal or converts. Orchid also
makes its broader assistant promise concrete through message transcripts.
Both examples can reduce conceptual risk before asking for a privileged
mailbox relationship.

Tecotype should create a faithful no-account marketing demonstration:

```text
source-tagged visitor
→ enters or selects the Copenhagen query
→ sees the cross-language match and why it matched
→ understands the local mailbox-data boundary
→ chooses the real evaluation route
```

The simulation must match actual behavior, be labeled as a demonstration, and
preserve source identity into the working client. Completion is an evaluation
signal; downstream first job and payment remain the decision metrics.

### 3. Coordinated launches leave durable surfaces, not customer proof

Zero's Product Hunt and open-source launch left the wave's largest visible
public footprint. Notion used an established audience and major press.
MonoMail earned a strong Reddit response. ReplylessAI used three smaller
Product Hunt launches; a small paid base also exists, but its original
acquisition sources are unknown.

None publishes a complete launch cohort through activation, payment, and
renewal. The transferable asset is the permanent bundle:

- canonical proof page;
- founder explanation;
- relevant community contribution;
- reviewer or launch listing;
- dated status and changelog; and
- unique source route for every placement.

Launch points, waitlist claims, stars, comments, and downloads should remain
diagnostics.

### 4. Public-state consistency is conversion work

Five clients expose lifecycle or public-state drift:

- Zero's email-client proof now routes into the broader Orchid assistant;
- Extra's homepage is open while its store copy still says invite-only;
- Airo's observed homepage states disagree on audience, features, tiers, and
  demo visibility;
- Notion's product page continues to promote the inbox while help and store
  surfaces disclose the scheduled closure; and
- Newton mixes mature trial/subscription, early access, old platform breadth,
  new store activity, and a missing changelog.

Marketing should maintain one current public-state registry for:

- platforms and providers;
- capabilities and limitations;
- price, trial, renewal, cancellation, and refund;
- privacy and data flow;
- current CTA and availability;
- change history;
- support; and
- continuity/export terms.

Every landing page, store, comparison, reviewer brief, partner listing, and
announcement should use that state. Broken or contradictory public proof can
raise acquisition friction even when the client works.

### 5. Search is mostly brand retrieval or noise

Wave 3 supplies little evidence for broad organic acquisition:

- ReplylessAI has 11 modeled keywords and 7.5 ETV, mostly from generic archive
  and migration articles.
- Airo has two keywords and 1.932 ETV.
- MonoMail's owned domain has no modeled keywords.
- Newton's three brand or legacy terms supply 98.73% of its 132.40 ETV.
- Orchid's inherited domains rank mostly for ambiguous or brand-like terms.
- Extra's useful discovery is primarily branded.
- Notion's visibility benefits from the parent brand, while category and
  transition pages are increasingly owned by others.

Tecotype should publish one permanent page that closes a validated job or
switching decision. It should not build a broad educational inventory from
volume, automate competitor-page production, or treat brand rank as net-new
distribution.

### 6. Transition intent can be useful without becoming predatory

Notion's announced closure and Newton's history now sit beside visible
alternative and migration result ecosystems. The query wording indicates
plausible high intent because the reader may have a real deadline or trust
objection; public evidence does not establish query volume or conversion.

A Tecotype transition page should:

- state the exact current status and date;
- link to the competitor's official guidance;
- explain what stays, what must be saved, and what does not transfer;
- disclose affiliation;
- state who should not choose Tecotype;
- use only verified Tecotype claims; and
- preserve source identity through first value and payment.

Avoid countdown pressure, invented causality, unsupported import claims, and
claims that a scheduled shutdown has already occurred.

### 7. Lifecycle marketing can convert without proving acquisition

ReplylessAI's founder attributes one lifetime upgrade to a plain update email.
That supports a small test of factual, consented product communication. It does
not reveal how that customer was acquired.

Tecotype should keep two dimensions:

```text
original acquisition source
+ subsequent lifecycle touch
→ activation, return, payment, renewal
```

A source should not receive credit merely because the final email was
measurable. Likewise, the update email should not be described as a discovery
channel.

### 8. Public prices do not validate Tecotype's price

Airo's direct page displays $8-per-month Personal and $25-per-month Pro tiers,
both billed annually, without public paid adoption. Newton's historical price
and 40,000-subscriber claim did not expose sustainable economics.
ReplylessAI's verified revenue is small and mixes subscription and lifetime
outcomes. Extra and Notion are or were free at the inspected entry point;
MonoMail is free/open source with donations and an anecdotal one-off build
offer; Orchid's current public amount is unclear.

Nothing in the wave supports lowering or raising Tecotype's decided
**€149/year or €15/month**. Test whether the specific professional retrieval
and control outcome earns it.

## What does not establish distribution

Across Wave 3, none of these is sufficient:

- a Product Hunt rank or point count;
- a waitlist or early-access claim;
- a GitHub star, fork, contributor, or release count;
- cumulative release-asset downloads;
- store download bands, ratings, or reviews;
- launch press or a backlink;
- a tagged URL without downstream events;
- a demo completion or trial start;
- directory coverage;
- a free product;
- an advertised high price;
- an aggregate revenue number;
- a donation route;
- an observed ad creative; or
- one lifecycle conversion.

Each can support a hypothesis. In this workspace, partner distribution counts
only when a permanent pre-install partner surface can introduce Tecotype with
zero existing users and preserve attribution from first action through
payment. The broader source-to-retained-paid chain below is the acquisition
measurement standard.

## Recommended Tecotype marketing sequence

### 1. Establish the canonical message

Lead with the half-remembered, cross-language retrieval job. Use calm,
keyboard control, multiple accounts, and structural privacy as supporting
reasons to believe.

Test it against one control-oriented variant with the same demonstration,
price, audience, and downstream measurement.

### 2. Publish the evaluation and truth layer

Create:

- a faithful no-account retrieval demonstration;
- a concise data-flow and privacy page;
- a current platform/provider matrix;
- price and entitlement terms;
- a permanent changelog;
- a continuity/export explanation; and
- one canonical reviewer brief.

The working client and billing systems should return only the coarse events
marketing needs. No mailbox content belongs in marketing analytics.

### 3. Run a small tagged launch bundle

Use:

- one high-fit reviewer;
- one or two relevant workflow, privacy, or desktop communities;
- one launch surface only if its audience matches; and
- one canonical page with a unique source token per placement.

Adapt the contribution to the community's actual question. Do not repeat one
promotional post across unrelated forums.

### 4. Add one high-intent owned page

Choose either:

- a job page for partial/cross-language email memory; or
- one factual comparison or transition page where Tecotype is genuinely
  relevant.

Continue only if the page produces qualified first-job and paid outcomes.

### 5. Test factual lifecycle communication

For consented evaluators, send a brief factual update. Preserve original
source and record the message as a separate touch. Compare first-job
completion, return, and payment against a holdout.

### 6. Scale only from source-to-retained-paid evidence

Use one shared funnel:

```text
permanent public source
→ qualified landing visit
→ demonstration or evaluation handoff
→ working client reports first lead job
→ voluntary return
→ billing reports payment
→ retained paid milestone
```

Marketing owns the source, message, public surface, channel cost, and decision
rule. Product delivery remains out of scope.

## Avoid

- generic AI-email or productivity positioning;
- using calm as a standalone search keyword;
- broad directory submission or content factories;
- mobile acquisition;
- launch attention without a permanent canonical page;
- repository, store, vote, waitlist, or download vanity accounting;
- category or domain changes that discard source continuity;
- stale access, platform, price, or privacy copy;
- fear-based shutdown marketing;
- unverified migration or integration claims;
- copying a competitor's visible price;
- forced sent-mail branding, contact scraping, automatic invitations, or
  recipient tracking; and
- paid-media or referral scale before the activation-to-paid chain is
  attributable.

## Evidence boundary

The dossiers strongly evidence public messages, launch surfaces, stores,
repository and release metadata, current search estimates, backlink indexes,
visible ad creatives, offer states, and documented shutdown or transition
events.

They do not expose comparable unique visitors, evaluation cohorts, connected
mailboxes, first-value completion, voluntary return, current paid users,
retention, renewals, refunds, CAC, support load, contribution margin, or
source-level economics. Those remain unknown rather than negative.
