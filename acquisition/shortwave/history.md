## Positioning evolution

### 1. Calm Google Inbox successor

The launch problem was cognitive overload: messages fall away, SaaS
notifications accumulate, and long threads become unclear. The answer was not
“work faster” alone. Shortwave promised “Inbox Organized,” bundles, todos, and
calm control.

This wedge was specific, emotionally coherent, and easy for Google Inbox
refugees to recognize.

### 2. AI executive assistant

Summaries created a timely entry into generative AI. The 2023 Assistant then
used an executive-assistant metaphor to explain a broader set of capabilities:
read, search, write, reason, schedule, and remember.

This widened the addressable problem and supplied repeated launch material. It
also shifted differentiation toward models and cloud processing that many
competitors can claim.

### 3. AI-native team inbox

The 2024 team launches made external-email collaboration a central business
case. Shared threads, comments, assignees, labels, templates, and per-seat
plans support land-and-expand acquisition.

The current homepage uses company logos and team outcomes much more heavily
than the 2022 launch.

### 4. Agent plus automation ecosystem

MCP integrations and Tasklet extend the story beyond email. Shortwave handles
interactive work; Tasklet handles triggers and background automation.

This creates more value and a sister-product funnel, but it makes the homepage
less singular than the original Google Inbox replacement.

## What stayed consistent

Four elements survived the positioning changes:

1. **Gmail as the system of record.** Shortwave still requires Gmail or Google
   Workspace for direct sign-in.
2. **Inbox organization.** Bundles, done, snooze, stars, todos, and delivery
   schedules remain visible.
3. **A taught workflow.** The Shortwave Method converts features into a
   repeatable triage practice.
4. **Frequent public launches.** Product Hunt, launch weeks, blog posts,
   changelog entries, and press provide recurring acquisition moments.

## Platform expansion and evidence boundary

Shortwave now publicly claims web, iOS, Android, Mac, and Windows. That is a
first-party availability statement and was corroborated across current
homepage and migration documentation. This research did not obtain or test any
client.

The funding, points, comments, rankings, release counts, and feature totals in
this timeline use different sources and definitions. None provides a complete
series for active accounts, retained teams, paid seats, or revenue.

## Historical lesson for Tecotype

Shortwave's strongest early story closely resembles Tecotype's emotional north
star: bring order to overload and return control. Shortwave then followed
market demand into cloud AI, collaboration, and automation.

Tecotype should not respond by presenting future MCP or agent work as current.
Its differentiated sequence is to release a trustworthy Mac-first,
keyboard-controlled Gmail-and-IMAP workflow, prove calm first value, and only
then broaden. Windows needs its own completed release gate before any public
availability claim.
