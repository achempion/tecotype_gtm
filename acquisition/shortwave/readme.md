# Shortwave acquisition report

Research date: **2026-07-17**

Market: **Google US-English, desktop where applicable**

Scope: **Shortwave's public acquisition history, current discovery surfaces,
and public funnel**

Research used public webpages, public metadata, and search-provider responses
only. No Shortwave client artifact was downloaded, fetched, installed,
mounted, opened, inspected, or executed. No account, mailbox, form, trial, or
checkout was used.

## Executive answer

Shortwave's strongest evidenced acquisition model is:

> Use a founder story and a recognizable Google Inbox replacement to win a
> concentrated productivity audience, teach a distinct inbox method, then
> create recurring launches around AI, team collaboration, and automation
> while a free plan, sent-mail signature, migration guides, and teammate
> invitations keep distribution inside the product.

The initial public launch produced **224 Hacker News points and 123 comments**
and a **#4 Product Hunt product-of-the-day** result. Those are attention
signals, not users. The more durable evidence is structural:
[repeated launches and coverage](./referrals.md),
[a taught triage workflow and switching guides](./funnel.md), and a current
[no-card free funnel with instrumented source continuity](./landing-page.md).

The current message is much broader than the 2022 launch. “Stay calm, focused,
and in control” became “Automate your email with AI.” Shortwave now sells an
AI agent, inbox control, team collaboration, model tiers, and a Tasklet
automation cross-sell. Its direct provider support remains Gmail/Google
Workspace; other supported providers require a Gmail forwarding bridge, and
Microsoft 365/Exchange is not supported.

Current DataForSEO estimates show **452 organic keywords**, **1,671.77 modeled
organic ETV**, **11,258 backlinks**, and **2,035 referring domains**. The
homepage ranks first for `shortwave email`, fifth in absolute placement for
`ai powered email client`, and the owned Google Inbox replacement ranks third
in absolute placement for `inbox by gmail alternative`. Shortwave did not
appear in the returned top ten for `email batching app`.

These figures establish discovery surfaces, not acquisition performance. No
public source exposes source-level signup, inbox connection, activation,
retention, paid conversion, renewal, or profitability.

## Acquisition eras

### Original model

```text
Firebase reputation / HN / Product Hunt / press
→ Google Inbox replacement for Gmail
→ free start or $9/month
→ bundles, todos, channels, and Shortwave Method
→ sent-mail visibility and word of mouth
```

### Current model

```text
Brand/category search / comparisons / content / stores / launches / ads
→ AI-agent and business-team landing page
→ no-card Free or 14-day business trial
→ Gmail authorization and sync
→ AI organization plus taught triage
→ Pro or per-seat intelligence tier
→ teammate expansion and Tasklet automation
```

The public record does not connect either chain to retained revenue.

## What changed

- The original calm Google Inbox successor became an AI agent for business
  email, while bundles, todos, schedules, and keyboard control stayed.
- Repeated feature launches turned product velocity into an acquisition
  calendar.
- Individual freemium expanded into per-seat model/intelligence tiers and team
  collaboration.
- Tasklet separated automatic background work from interactive inbox work and
  created a sister-product funnel.
- Platform claims widened to web, iOS, Android, Mac, and Windows, but direct
  provider support stayed anchored to Gmail/Google Workspace.
- Inbound tracker blocking now sits beside outbound read statuses, recent
  opens, and link tracking—a trust tension Tecotype should not copy.

## Implications for Tecotype

- **What appears to work for public discovery and evaluation:** Shortwave joins a memorable switching story, founder/community credibility, a taught workflow, honest migration continuity, repeated launches, and product-native sharing; current search evidence also supports its brand, AI-email, and Google Inbox surfaces, but no public source attributes those routes through paid conversion.
- **What appears weak, discontinued, or inconclusive:** The original singular calm-control story is diluted by agent, team, model-tier, and Tasklet messages; broad writing content and AI directories have uncertain product intent; the Gmail bridge constrains provider breadth; no public source exposes source-level activation, retention, renewal, or profitability.
- **What Tecotype should test:** Validate one calm-control job now; after the Mac release gate, guide a small source-tagged cohort to first value, encode the repeated guidance into a reversible workflow, and test one truthful Gmail-plus-IMAP switching page plus one small founder launch; repeat separately only after Windows reaches its own gate.
- **What Tecotype should avoid:** Do not claim an AI agent or unshipped platform, copy Inbox Zero pressure, force a sent-mail signature, surveil recipients, build broad low-intent SEO, add a multi-pixel stack prematurely, or confuse launch votes, backlinks, and modeled traffic with customers.
- **Evidence strength and remaining unknowns:** Launch dates, HN/Product Hunt attention, public messaging, prices, provider limits, migration guides, scripts, current SERPs, backlinks, and ad archives are well evidenced; qualified traffic, login and OAuth completion, sync, first value, voluntary return, payment, team expansion, churn, support cost, CAC, LTV, and profit remain private.
