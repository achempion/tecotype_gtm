# Staged Shortwave-informed opportunities for Tecotype

Research date: **2026-07-17**

## Platform and release truth

Tecotype is under active development.

- **Mac:** an Apple Silicon signed-release target exists, but local builds are
  testing-only and the public release remains gated by onboarding, website,
  licensing/payment, Gmail audit, signing/notarization, updater,
  distribution, safe compose/send, and measurement work. A build target is not
  public availability.
- **Windows:** packaging, signing, updates, distribution, and release
  validation remain planned. Windows has not shipped.
- **MCP and agent automation:** future work, not a current product capability.

The experiments below preserve those boundaries.

## Transferable model

Shortwave's useful sequence is:

```text
Recognizable inbox pain
→ one memorable control mechanism
→ relevant community and founder story
→ low-risk switching and taught workflow
→ repeated feature launches
→ team/referral expansion after individual value
```

Tecotype can adopt the sequence without copying Gmail dependence, cloud-agent
positioning, forced branding, or recipient tracking.

## Experiments

### 1. Calm-control problem interviews

**Stage:** Now; no public client distribution.

**Hypothesis:** A narrow professional segment already tries to reduce inbox
interruptions, recover messages from partial memory, or work across Gmail and
another IMAP provider, and describes the desired outcome as control rather
than speed.

**Execution:** Interview 8–10 people about a recent incident, provider mix,
workaround, switching history, interruption pattern, and trust requirements.
Show only clearly labeled development material.

**Measure:** Independent repetitions of the same job, recency, current spend
or workaround, provider fit, and willingness to join a later invited Mac
cohort.

**Continue if:** At least five people independently report the same frequent
job and at least three recently tried to solve it. **Stop or narrow if:** the
job is rare, aspirational, or depends on future MCP/automation.

**Ethic:** Do not promise access, a launch date, Windows, or an AI assistant.

### 2. Two honest pre-release messages

**Stage:** Now; research or notification page only.

**Hypothesis:** A concrete calm-control promise qualifies better prospects
than an AI-agent or inbox-zero promise.

**Execution:** Test:

- “Find and finish important email without inbox pressure.”
- “One keyboard-controlled inbox for Gmail and IMAP.”

Keep the same development-state label and use a research-conversation or
notification action, not Download.

**Measure:** Qualified conversation intent, provider/platform fit, repeated
job language, and privacy questions—not clicks alone.

**Continue if:** One version produces at least five qualified conversations
and a clearer shared job. **Stop if:** interest depends on an unshipped feature
or vague “AI” curiosity.

**Ethic:** No time-saved number, fake availability, urgency, or manufactured
waitlist position.

### 3. Founder-guided Mac first value

**Stage:** Only after the Mac gate is complete for a small invited cohort.

**Hypothesis:** Guiding one calm workflow—connect, sync, find or triage, then
return—improves first value and exposes onboarding gaps.

**Execution:** Invite a small source-tagged Apple Silicon cohort. Let people
choose guided or self-serve setup and record the selection bias. Use synthetic
examples where possible and focus on one job rather than a tour.

**Measure:** Account connected, first sync, first useful search/triage action,
undo/error rate, voluntary day-one and day-seven return, support minutes, and
trust objections.

**Continue if:** At least five non-team users reach first value and at least
three return on day seven without manual repair. **Stop if:** success depends
on unsafe sending, access to private mail, or hiding product gaps.

**Ethic:** Obtain explicit consent before viewing any inbox content.

### 4. Productize a reversible Tecotype Method

**Stage:** After guided sessions reveal repeated friction.

**Hypothesis:** A short, optional workflow can teach keyboard control without
Shortwave's pressure to triage every message first.

**Execution:** Teach only the minimum steps for the validated job in a safe
practice state. Explain local storage and provider sync, offer “Later,” and
make every real-mail action reversible.

**Measure:** First-value completion, abandonment, error/undo, support,
accessibility, and day-seven return.

**Continue if:** Completion and return improve without higher errors or
pressure complaints. **Stop if:** metrics rise only because people are trapped
or pushed into mass action.

**Ethic:** Calm control means agency. Do not shame a messy inbox or make Inbox
Zero the definition of success.

### 5. One provider-breadth switching page

**Stage:** After the Mac public release is genuinely available.

**Hypothesis:** A factual page for people who use Gmail plus Fastmail, iCloud,
or another IMAP provider will attract higher-fit demand than generic “AI email
client” content.

**Execution:** Publish one dated page explaining released Gmail and generic
IMAP support, local working state, account limits, known provider caveats, and
the exact Mac scope. A Shortwave comparison is appropriate only if current,
fair, and useful.

**Measure:** Search impressions diagnostically; source-tagged account
connection, first value, day-seven return, and paid conversion as the decision
metrics.

**Continue if:** The page produces qualified activation. **Stop if:** it
attracts support traffic for providers Tecotype does not reliably support.

**Ethic:** Do not call Shortwave “Gmail-only” without explaining its forwarding
workarounds, and do not call Tecotype cross-platform.

### 6. Calm batching-language content test

**Stage:** After release, and only around implemented behavior.

**Hypothesis:** Shortwave's absence from the current `email batching app`
top ten leaves room for a calm-control explanation that maps to released
Tecotype Split Inboxes, reminders, and triage.

**Execution:** Publish one practical workflow page about reducing inbox
interruption. Avoid claiming delivery schedules or notification batching
unless Tecotype actually ships them. Demonstrate the product rather than
producing generic productivity advice.

**Measure:** Qualified product actions and return, not raw informational
traffic.

**Continue if:** Readers reach a released first-value action. **Stop if:** the
page ranks for advice but sends no qualified evaluation.

**Ethic:** No pseudo-scientific productivity or time-saved claims.

### 7. Repeated small launches, not one giant launch

**Stage:** After a reliable Mac release and retained cohort.

**Hypothesis:** One founder-led launch for the validated job, followed by
specific release notes for meaningful improvements, will compound relevant
attention more safely than a large launch.

**Execution:** Start with the community closest to the validated job. State
affiliation, Apple Silicon scope, provider support, price, data path, and known
limits. Carry a durable first-party source identifier through activation.

**Measure:** Source → first launch → connection → sync → first value →
day-one/day-seven return → paid. Treat votes and mentions as diagnostics.

**Continue if:** At least five non-team users reach first value and at least
three return on day seven. **Stop if:** launch attention exposes signing,
OAuth, sync, update, or trust failures.

**Ethic:** No vote manipulation, referral queue-jumping, or inflated “users”
claim.

### 8. Retention-gated, user-controlled referral

**Stage:** After paid conversion and renewal measurement are stable.

**Hypothesis:** Satisfied users can introduce other high-fit professionals
without mandatory sent-mail branding.

**Execution:** Offer a modest service credit only after the referred user
reaches a retained-use milestone. Keep selection and sending entirely under
the user's control.

**Measure:** Referred first-value and return, paid conversion, credit cost,
complaints, and renewal versus a comparable direct cohort.

**Continue if:** Referred users retain at least as well and complaints remain
negligible. **Stop if:** it creates spam, low-intent signups, or hidden unit
economics.

**Ethic:** No mandatory signature, contact scraping, automatic invitations,
read receipts, link tracking, or recipient surveillance.

### 9. Privacy-minimal source continuity

**Stage:** Design now; enable only with the public acquisition surface.

**Hypothesis:** First-party source and activation events are enough to choose
early channels without Shortwave's multi-pixel footprint.

**Execution:** Record a short-lived source identifier and the smallest complete
funnel: qualified visit, acquisition action, first launch, connection, sync,
first value, voluntary return, and payment. Document retention and deletion.

**Measure:** Event completeness and cohort decisions, not the number of tags.

**Continue if:** The data changes a channel decision. **Remove or redesign if:**
an event is unused, unreliable, or cannot be explained plainly.

**Ethic:** Do not send mailbox content, recipient behavior, or unnecessary
identifiers into marketing analytics.

### 10. Separate Windows release cohort

**Stage:** Only after Windows packaging, signing, update, first-run, provider,
payment, and safe-send gates are complete.

**Hypothesis:** The validated job transfers to Windows, but shortcuts,
distribution trust, and activation friction differ.

**Execution:** Re-run the guided and self-serve learning cycle using Windows
copy, Ctrl-based shortcuts, screenshots, and a separate source tag.

**Measure:** The same first-value and return chain, segmented by OS, installer
and update success, provider, and accessibility configuration.

**Continue if:** Windows independently meets release-quality and return
thresholds. **Stop if:** Mac evidence is being used to excuse Windows-specific
failures.

**Ethic:** Say “planned” until a tested Windows release actually ships.

## What to avoid

- Do not lead with “AI agent” or Tasklet-like automation; Tecotype does not
  currently ship that product.
- Do not copy a mandatory sent-mail signature or any recipient tracking.
- Do not use Inbox Zero, shame, urgency, or forced mass triage as activation.
- Do not build a broad library of rejection and thank-you articles before one
  high-intent page connects to retained use.
- Do not equate launch votes, backlinks, modeled ETV, or waitlist entries with
  active customers.
- Do not add Google, X, and Meta pixels before a privacy-minimal first-party
  funnel proves insufficient.
- Do not describe Mac or Windows as publicly available until each platform's
  own release gate is complete.
