over copying a broad email-writing library.

## Brand mentions

A Content Analysis query for exact phrase `Shortwave email` reported **13,143**
possible citations and returned 100. The sample included TechCrunch, Apple
podcast and store surfaces, Droid Life, Zapier, Android publications,
AlternativeTo, AI directories, scraped copies, and third-party app-package
sites.

The count is not a clean earned-media total. It includes duplicates, syndicated
or copied content, store pages, directory entries, and unsafe/unofficial
package sites. This research did not visit, obtain, or inspect any client
artifact from those results.

## Paid-search and ad evidence

The Labs snapshot contained no current paid keywords. A Google Ads Advertisers
lookup for exact phrase `shortwave email` returned task status `40102 No Search
Results`.

A separate Google Ads Transparency creative search for `shortwave.com`,
covering **2018-05-31 through 2026-07-17**, returned **25 creatives** associated
with advertiser ID `AR16707747034290978817` and title **Shortwave
Communications, Inc.**:

- 21 text;
- 3 image; and
- 1 video.

In the returned sample, the earliest `first_shown` value was
**2024-08-15**, and the latest `last_shown` value was **2026-05-08**. The
archive proves that corporate creatives existed. It does not expose spend,
impressions, targeting, incrementality, or whether an ad was active on the
research date.

The apparently conflicting results are different lookups: the advertiser
endpoint searched by phrase, while the creative archive searched by domain.

## Search conclusion

Shortwave has three defensible search strengths: brand control, first-page
visibility for an AI-email category term, and a durable Google Inbox
replacement page. Broad writing-advice posts add reach, but their path to
activated client use is unobservable. The missing top-ten result for `email
batching app` shows that a central product behavior does not automatically
translate into ownership of the user's plain-language problem.
