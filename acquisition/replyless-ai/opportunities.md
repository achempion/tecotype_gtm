# ReplylessAI-derived opportunities for Tecotype

Scope: **marketing and distribution only**. Assume the Tecotype client works
and product delivery is handled elsewhere. Assume published Tecotype supports
macOS, Windows, and Linux; campaign copy must still match the public release
routes verified at campaign time.

## 1. Source-preserving founder launch

### Hypothesis

A founder-led launch built around one demonstrable job will create fewer but
more qualified evaluations than a broad “AI email client” announcement.

### Test

Choose one proof:

- find a message from partial or cross-language memory;
- process a representative inbox from the keyboard; or
- turn a crowded inbox into calm, inspectable queues.

Publish the same proof on a permanent launch page and two or three specialist
surfaces. Give every source its own tag and preserve original acquisition
source through payment and renewal.

### Readout

Compare qualified visit, demo completion, activation, voluntary return,
payment, and renewal by source. Do not optimize for points, impressions, or
comments alone.

## 2. A small, audited directory portfolio

ReplylessAI’s directory activity plausibly generated signups, but 98.8% of its
indexed referring pages are nofollow and the founder does not name the useful
directories.

Test at most five listings selected for audience fit and permanent discovery.
Record:

- listing URL and upkeep cost;
- tag preserved at landing;
- qualified visits;
- activated and paid users; and
- founder time spent maintaining stale copy and pricing.

Retain only surfaces that produce attributable activated-paid outcomes or
credible reviewer conversations.

## 3. Plain lifecycle update email

ReplylessAI reports one lifetime upgrade from an email that only said what
shipped and what was coming.

For a consented Tecotype cohort, test a short factual update against no update.
Keep acquisition source immutable and record the update as a separate lifecycle
touch. Measure return, evaluation completion, and payment after the update.

The mailing list must remain separate from mailbox data. Tecotype
infrastructure never receives user credentials or mailbox contents, and
marketing should not imply that it mines mail to time messages.

## 4. One high-intent comparison or workflow page

ReplylessAI built many feature and use-case pages, but current search value
comes mainly from generic migration and archive guides.

Test one page around a verified buying decision, such as a calm alternative to
performance-oriented premium email or local Better search for multilingual
retrieval. Include:

- an exact product demonstration;
- clear audience exclusions;
- current platform availability;
- annual and monthly price in the same form as checkout; and
- one next action.

Do not scale production until the page yields source-tagged activation and
payment.

## 5. Public evidence without vanity accounting

ReplylessAI usefully reports users, processed emails, ARR, revenue, and current
subscriptions, but none forms a source-level cohort.

If Tecotype publishes progress, prefer a small complete cohort:

```text
source-tagged evaluations
→ activated users
→ voluntary day-seven return
→ paid users
→ renewals when observable
```

Avoid using indexed links, emails processed, page views, or aggregate revenue
as a substitute.

## Avoid

- Broad directory submission before audience-message fit.
- Generic AI-email articles whose query intent is support rather than switching.
- Unsourced speed or time-saving multipliers.
- Different prices in metadata, directories, marketing pages, and checkout.
- Treating a lifecycle conversion as proof of the acquisition source.
- Claiming macOS, Windows, or Linux availability before each public route is
  actually live, even though future published Tecotype is assumed to support
  all three desktop platforms.

## Evidence threshold

ReplylessAI justifies testing a founder launch, a small directory portfolio,
and plain update email. It does not justify scaling any of them. Scale requires
permanent discovery plus an attributable activation-to-paid funnel.
