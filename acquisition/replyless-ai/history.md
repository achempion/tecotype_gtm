## Message evolution

The first launch message was affordability plus AI-sorted inbox zero: Product
Hunt explicitly contrasted the product with expensive subscriptions and
command-line email workflows. The next two launches added Telegram briefs and
outbound sequences, broadening the category beyond the core email client.

The current homepage shell broadens the message to:

- Gmail, Outlook, and Zoho coverage;
- focused views for founders, creators, freelancers, agencies, and teams;
- drafts, summaries, reminders, snippets, daily briefs, and cleaner workflows;
- “calmer” inbox operation; and
- sequences, CRM workflows, shared inboxes, and role-specific use cases.

This creates more acquisition entry points, but moves the product from one
simple promise toward a wide email operating system.

## Pricing changes and disagreement

Public sources preserve at least three structures:

1. Early directory pages such as [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/10038711/Replyless/)
   describe Free, $8 Pro, $19 Mega, and $83 Agency plans.
2. The founder’s July
   [LinkedIn post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/srivatsamudumby_i-quit-a-high-paying-job-to-build-software-activity-7471048641974292480-RSwh)
   describes $9 and $49 plans with no free tier.
3. The current pricing SEO shell describes a $9 Basics plan, Pro, Lifetime,
   seven-day trials, and 30% annual savings, while
   [TrustMRR](https://trustmrr.com/startup/replylessai) lists $15, $30, and
   $299.

Some differences may be stale directories, billing-period presentation, or
active experimentation. The rendered pricing page was unavailable, so the
exact dates and current interactive prices cannot be established. What is
observable is substantial public inconsistency within four months of launch.

Outlook migration and Gmail archive queries rather than the newer role pages.

## Archive limitation

The Internet Archive CDX index returned only two unique 2026 Replyless URLs,
both captured in May, and did not preserve the March launch page in the queried
index. No historical page content was used to infer unobserved changes.

## Tecotype lesson

Repeated, factual distribution moments can compound, but message and price
changes need a durable public source of truth. Tecotype should keep launch
offers, pricing metadata, directory listings, comparison pages, and checkout
copy synchronized before amplifying them.
