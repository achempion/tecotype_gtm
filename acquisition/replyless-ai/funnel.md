# ReplylessAI public funnel

Captured: **2026-07-17**

No account, trial, mailbox connection, provider authorization, checkout, or
form was started. The map below joins public offer evidence with the founder’s
reported outcomes and marks the missing steps.

## Public path

```text
Product Hunt, directory, founder post, exact-brand search, or owned guide
→ replyless.ai landing or feature page
→ pricing or account CTA in the rendered application
→ seven-day Basics or Pro trial, according to the pricing shell
→ account creation and Gmail / Outlook / Zoho connection
→ AI sorting, split inbox, draft, summary, or cleaner first value
→ paid Basics, Pro, or Lifetime plan
→ continued use and renewal
```

The first two steps are publicly visible. The rendered CTA and all steps after
it were deliberately not exercised.

## Offer evidence

The public [pricing page](https://www.replyless.ai/pricing) initial HTML states:

- Basics and Pro include a seven-day free trial;
- Basics is $9 per month in its SEO shell;
- annual billing saves 30%;
- Basics covers one connected inbox;
- Pro covers more accounts, history, and volume; and
- Lifetime is a one-time option within product limits.

The interactive cards could not be rendered. Other public sources show
different price structures; see [landing-page.md](./landing-page.md) and
[history.md](./history.md). The existence of a trial lowers evaluation friction,
but no public trial-start or trial-to-paid rate is available.

## Public paid evidence

### Founder report

The founder’s
[three-month account](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/srivatsamudumby_i-quit-a-high-paying-job-to-build-software-activity-7471048641974292480-RSwh)
reported:

- 72 users and 11,000 processed emails at month two;
- $600 ARR at month two;
- $1,000 in revenue by month three; and
- one lifetime upgrade within hours of a factual product-update email.

This proves a claimed user and payment loop. “Processed emails” is an activity
count, not unique activation or retention. The post does not define a user,
state how many connected an inbox, report cohort return, or join the lifetime
buyer to their original acquisition source.

### Verified revenue surface

[TrustMRR](https://trustmrr.com/startup/replylessai) reported on July 12:

- $3,988 all-time revenue;
- $78 MRR; and
- four active subscriptions.

TrustMRR says the revenue is verified with a Stripe API key. That is credible
payment evidence. It does not disclose plan mix, refunds, churn, acquisition
source, activated accounts, or whether lifetime payments are excluded from
MRR.

## What is attributable

| Claim | Attribution level |
| --- | --- |
| Product Hunt generated feedback | Founder-reported, no quantified signup or payment link |
| A few directories generated steady signups | Founder-reported, directories not named or separated |
| One update email generated a lifetime upgrade | Direct lifecycle conversion attribution |
| 72 users / $600 ARR / $1,000 revenue | Aggregate cohort outcome, no source attribution |
| $3,988 all-time / $78 MRR / four subscriptions | Stripe-verified aggregate payment state |

The strongest causal fragment starts with an existing early user receiving an
update email. Because the update email is neither the original acquisition
source nor permanent public discovery, it is a lifecycle conversion tactic,
not a complete distribution channel under Tecotype’s definition.

## Missing economics

The following remain unobservable:

- visitor volume by source;
- account and provider-connection completion;
- first successful inbox classification, draft, or search;
- day-one, day-seven, and month-one return;
- trial-to-paid conversion;
- renewal, churn, refunds, and support burden;
- CAC and founder time by directory; and
- payment and retention by original discovery source.

## Tecotype measurement adaptation

```text
permanent specialist listing, review, launch, or high-intent page
→ source-tagged segment landing page
→ concrete workflow demonstration
→ evaluation
→ account connected and first promised job completed
→ voluntary return
→ payment
→ renewal
```

Product delivery is outside this workspace, but marketing attribution should
preserve the original source through the downstream events. Lifecycle update
email should be a separate field, not allowed to overwrite acquisition source.
