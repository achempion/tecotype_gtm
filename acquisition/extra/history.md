# Extra acquisition history

This timeline distinguishes shipped public surfaces from reported metrics and planned work.

## Timeline

|---|---|---|

| 2026-04-21 | Public launch with controlled access | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/21/former-pinterest-team-redesigns-email-with-extra-and-its-actually-good/) reports Gmail-only iOS/web availability, a waitlist, invites, and special codes. |

## Narrative evolution

The launch narrative began with exclusivity and founder-network codes, using controlled access to create urgency and pace onboarding. The current homepage has shifted to universal availability and a broad free offer. Paid creative narrows the story again—not by reinstating access scarcity, but by advertising a single immediate job: Gmail cleanup.

That is a plausible evolution from:

```text
beta proof
  -> invite-gated launch
  -> coordinated social/editorial codes
  -> open free access
  -> job-specific paid acquisition
```

The sequence is **observed**; whether each step caused growth is **unobservable**.

## Surface debt

The App Store's obsolete waitlist language is the clearest historical residue. The homepage also exposes a Mac continuation label while the founder describes native Mac as a beta opening during summer. Marketing should distinguish current general availability, current beta availability, and future plans at every route.

## Reliability signal

One June App Store reviewer said the interface was appealing but missed urgent invoices and reservations and that they returned to Gmail; the developer replied in July that reliability matters and cited improvements. This is one anecdote, not a representative sample. It does illustrate the unusually high proof burden created by an action-first inbox: the marketing promise is only durable if users trust what is surfaced and what is deprioritized.

## Historical unknowns

Waitlist size, invite-to-activation rate, code performance, beta retention, launch-cohort retention, paid-media spend, and current active users were not found. No historical activation-to-paid funnel is visible because the offer is free and monetization remains undisclosed.
