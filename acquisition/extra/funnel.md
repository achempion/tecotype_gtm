# Extra public acquisition funnel

This maps only public marketing transitions. No app, installer, web app, login, OAuth, account, mailbox, form, or checkout route was entered.

## Current funnel

```text
Branded search / press / social / creator / paid cleanup creative
    -> extra.email or a tagged Extra URL
    -> "Get the app" / iOS / web / Mac-beta-labelled route
    -> Gmail connection and product onboarding
    -> first useful action
    -> retained use
    -> unknown future monetization
```

## Step-by-step evidence

| Stage | Public evidence | State | Attribution limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Discovery | Press, App Store, social results, referral parameters, launch codes, two recent ad creatives | Observed | Reach, cost, and incremental lift unknown |
| Landing | Current homepage explains Today, cleanup, categories, voice, and multiple accounts | Observed | No first-party click or experiment data |
| Continuation | Primary App Store link plus labels for iOS, web, and Mac | Observed labels only | Routes were not followed; Mac is described by a founder as beta |
| Access | Founder says waitlist is gone; store copy still says invite-only | Conflicting current surfaces | Drop-off from the mismatch unknown |
| Gmail connection | Public modal text explains OAuth and trust claims | Observed marketing copy | Connection flow and completion not inspected |
| Activation | A plausible first useful action is seeing a useful Today summary or completing cleanup | Inferred | Extra does not publish an activation definition or rate |
| Retention | Frequent App Store releases and company-reported aggregate beta activity | Weak proxy | No retention cohort, DAU/MAU, or source-level data |
| Payment | Homepage and store say free; monetization is undisclosed | No public payment step | Activation-to-paid attribution cannot currently be observed |

## Launch funnel change

At launch, access was deliberately constrained. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/21/former-pinterest-team-redesigns-email-with-extra-and-its-actually-good/) reported a waitlist, invites, and special codes. [Upstarts](https://www.upstartsmedia.com/p/extra-consumer-ai-email-app-launches) paired editorial coverage with a limited `UPSTARTS` access code. Current founder reporting says the waitlist is now gone. [Source](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/steveram_we-shipped-a-ton-in-june-at-buildforever-activity-7478165171614457856-6WV7)

Codes could have helped pace onboarding and associate a source with access. No public evidence shows that source attribution persisted beyond code redemption.

## Offer economics

Extra is currently offered free. Launch reporting says the founders did not expect a billion consumers to pay a standard monthly email subscription and were still exploring monetization. [Upstarts](https://www.upstartsmedia.com/p/extra-consumer-ai-email-app-launches) mentions possible future premium assistant, affiliate, or brand-placement ideas; those are discussion and speculation, not shipped offers.

Consequently:

- an install or Gmail connection is not a paid conversion;
- App Store ratings cannot be interpreted as customers;
- tracked referrals can show attribution intent without proving a business-quality cohort;
- the public funnel ends before an observable economic event.

## Friction and trust hypotheses

The following are **inferences to test**, not measured facts:

- A specific cleanup promise may convert cold traffic better than the broader "life inbox" category.
- The claim that Extra analyzes every email raises privacy and reliability questions at the OAuth step.
- Conflicting open-access and waitlist copy can create avoidable uncertainty.
- Multi-account consumers may have higher pain and higher retention than single-account users.
- A free offer may improve first-step conversion while making qualification for future payment harder.

## What would prove distribution

A defensible channel claim needs a permanent public source route, a persistent campaign/source ID, a predeclared activation event, retention by acquisition cohort, and a paid event or other explicit economic outcome. None of that end-to-end evidence is public today.
