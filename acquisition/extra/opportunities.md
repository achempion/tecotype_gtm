# Marketing opportunities for Tecotype from Extra

These are acquisition experiments, not product commitments. They assume Tecotype's published client and supported integrations are available as described elsewhere; every claim and route must be verified before publication.

## 1. Test an action-first landing page

**Job:** "Show me what needs attention today without making me surrender control."

Create a permanent page around a calm daily triage outcome. Demonstrate how attention is surfaced, how a user can inspect the reason, and what happens when the system is uncertain. This adapts Extra's legible Today framing while differentiating on predictability and control.

**Discovery:** job-specific search and small paid/social tests for overloaded personal email.

**Measure:** source-tagged landing visit → supported-client start → first completed triage → retained use at day 7/30 → paid conversion. Predeclare the activation event and do not call impressions, clicks, or starts distribution.

**Gate:** do not scale until the page-to-paid cohort is attributable and the reliability claim matches shipped behavior.

## 2. Test inbox cleanup as the narrow wedge

Extra's observed creative turns a broad assistant into a concrete Gmail-cleanup job. Tecotype can test a privacy-explicit version of that problem shape, such as reducing recurring newsletter noise while keeping the user in control.

Run a bounded campaign against one permanent landing page, with:

- one clear job and one verifiable outcome;
- precise local-processing and mailbox-data language;
- a small cap and a holdout or channel comparison;
- persistent campaign tags through activation and payment;
- no implication that Tecotype can identify importance perfectly.

Compare activation and paid conversion with the broader action-first page, not just click-through rate.

## 3. Use source-specific partner routes only when attribution survives

Founder, editorial, newsletter, and creator routes can be useful when each points to a durable page and a source ID persists through the full funnel. A short code alone is insufficient.

Test a small group of partners whose audiences have a specific email job. Report:

- eligible visits;
- first useful action;
- retention;
- paid conversion;
- refunds or early churn;
- source-level confidence and sample size.

Stop any partner route that produces attention without a business-quality cohort.

## 4. Turn reliability into acquisition proof

An action-first inbox creates fear of missed obligations. Marketing can address that fear directly with a short, verifiable demonstration of uncertainty, fallback, and user correction. Use real interface evidence only when it matches the current release.

Possible proof assets:

- a transparent example showing why an item is surfaced;
- a comparison of default and user-adjusted triage;
- a clear statement of what the system does not decide;
- a public reliability/change log tied to marketing claims.

Do not turn private mailbox contents into public proof.

## 5. Keep platform and access copy synchronized

Extra's open-access homepage, stale invite-only store text, and Mac-beta wording show how launch residue can create conversion friction. Maintain one release-status source for every landing page, store listing, comparison page, and partner brief. Label general availability, beta, and planned work plainly.

## Avoid

- a vague "AI knows what matters" promise without controllability proof;
- mobile-first claims or routes outside Tecotype's scope;
- free-consumer acquisition without a defined economic hypothesis;
- treating ratings, backlinks, launch coverage, or waitlist size as paid distribution;
- temporary creator codes that cannot be tied to activation and payment;
- publishing an integration, platform, or reliability claim before current builds are verified.
