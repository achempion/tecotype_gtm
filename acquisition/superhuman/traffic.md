## Ranked-keyword sample

The request returned 250 keywords ordered by search volume. Their combined
modeled ETV was **36,680.91**, so all percentages below describe that returned
sample rather than the domain total.

| Slice of the returned sample | Modeled ETV | Share of returned ETV | Interpretation |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Exact literal keyword `superhuman` | 18,392.00 | 50.14% | Strong brand visibility, but the term now spans the suite |
| `blog.superhuman.com` landing URLs | 14,515.78 | 39.57% | Broad informational content is visibly important |
| Conservatively classified Mail URLs | 1,917.78 | 5.23% | Direct Mail-path visibility is much smaller; homepage and blog can still assist Mail |

The exact query `superhuman` had search volume **33,100**, rank **1**, and
modeled ETV **10,062.40** to the homepage—27.43% of the returned sample. The
suite query `superhuman go` also ranked first, with volume **12,100** and ETV
**3,678.40**, illustrating why the domain can no longer be analyzed as a
Mail-only property.

High-volume informational examples included:

- `newsletter headline`: volume 246,000, rank 16, ETV 1,057.80;
- `macbook hacks`: volume 14,800, rank 4, ETV 975.32;
- `how to share google calendar`: volume 49,500, rank 12, ETV 371.25; and
- `ai email generator`: volume 18,100, rank 33, ETV 38.01.

The sample also contained Gmail and Outlook how-to queries, plus substantial
suite contamination from Coda, Grammarly, Go, community pages, and unrelated
terms. The visible pattern is a broad informational SEO operation, not a clean
set of high-intent email-client queries.

Four live top-ten Google US queries were inspected.

| Query | Superhuman result | Reading |

## Paid-search and ad evidence

DataForSEO Labs found three current paid keywords with only **7.27 modeled
ETV**, too little to establish paid search as a large current traffic source.

A Google Ads Advertisers lookup for `superhuman mail` returned API status
`40102` (“No Search Results”). A separate Google Ads Transparency search for
`superhuman.com`, covering **2018-05-31 through 2026-07-17**, returned the
maximum requested **40 creatives** from verified advertiser **Superhuman
Platform Inc.**: **36 video** and **4 text** results.

The results prove that the verified corporate advertiser has a Google creative
archive. They do not identify Mail-only spend, impressions, dates for the
returned sample, conversion, or incremental return. The company now advertises
multiple products, so even domain-matched creatives cannot automatically be
assigned to Mail.

The landing page also exposes Reddit, LinkedIn, Impact, Podscribe, G2, Dub, and
UTM instrumentation. Those tags establish measurement capability, not active
spend or channel performance.

## Search conclusion

The current domain has meaningful brand authority and a large broad-content
footprint. The clearest Mail-specific win is brand SERP control. The evidence
is much weaker for direct ownership of generic category queries, and the
suite rebrand makes domain totals increasingly unsuitable for Mail attribution.
For Tecotype, the useful pattern is message consistency and a small number of
high-intent proof pages—not copying a suite-scale editorial inventory.
