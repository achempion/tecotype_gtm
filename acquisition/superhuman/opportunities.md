# Staged opportunities for Tecotype

Research date: **2026-07-17**

Decision update: **2026-07-19**

Archive notice: the candidate tests below are historical; none is a current
**Experiment**. Use the [current decision](../../next-acquisition-funnel.md)

## Platform and release truth

Tecotype is under active development.

- **Mac:** a public Apple Silicon artifact was verified on 2026-07-19, but a
  trustworthy acquisition release is still gated by first run,
  licensing/payment, website, Gmail audit, signing/notarization/update
  readiness, safe compose/send, and measurement work. An artifact alone does
  not establish the full marketed product contract.
- **Windows:** distribution is planned and release-gated. It has not shipped.
- **Linux:** included in the GTM planning premise, but no public release was
  verified in this research.

The experiments below preserve that distinction. They do not turn current
development work, MCP ideas, or planned Windows distribution into acquisition
claims.

For acquisition planning, assume the eventual published client is ready on
macOS, Windows, and Linux, as directed. Every public page must still verify the
actual platform, provider, entitlement, and sender-safety claims at launch.

## Decision update: confirmed pain, rejected paid acquisition

The research confirmed a specific full-client switching job:

> A Superhuman user or evaluator with several active accounts wants to process
> all mail in one view instead of cycling account tabs.

Five cap-compliant current users or evaluators report this same mechanism
across Reddit, Capterra, and the App Store. Their behavior includes repeated
daily checking, missed work mail, paid use, cancellation/refund, long use of a
competing full client, and a reported $140/year Polymail purchase for ten
accounts. Superhuman's current
[Unified Inbox Workaround](https://help.superhuman.com/hc/en-us/articles/46005722297229-Unified-Inbox-Workaround)
confirms the tab workflow.

The offer must be a permanent free, fully actionable **All Accounts** client:
unlimited supported accounts; All Accounts and account-specific views;
read/reply/archive/move; ordinary cross-account search and shortcuts; and
visible, reliable sender identity. A read-only companion fails because it
preserves the account switching the user is trying to remove. Individual may
sell only distinct advanced workflows, not the core unified-inbox action.

The organic discovery and conversion hypothesis is:

```text
organic `superhuman unified inbox` result or peer recommendation
→ /superhuman-unified-inbox-alternative
→ dated factual comparison and All Accounts proof
→ desktop download
→ two supported accounts ready
→ one useful action in each account from All Accounts
→ repeated All Accounts processing
→ distinct advanced workflow
→ Individual purchase
→ retained paid milestone
```

DataForSEO modeled only 40 US searches/month at USD 15.19 CPC. At Tecotype's
0.2% working visitor-to-paid rate, media CAC is USD 7,595; at the cited 0.36%
external benchmark it is still USD 4,219. The query needs 30.38%
click-to-paid merely to reach the USD 50 gate, before non-media costs.
Paid search is therefore disqualified on both cost and capacity.

A dated comparison may remain as inexpensive organic content, but it is not
the next acquisition funnel. Spark and Mailspring already provide free
actionable unified inboxes, some users prefer account separation, and sender
mistakes are a hard stop. The updated status and source audit are in the
[confirmed-pain dossier](../../confirmed-pain-opportunities.md#superhuman-separates-active-accounts-into-tabs).

This remains an unproven Tecotype-owned organic hypothesis. No partner or
permanent pre-install surface with an attributable zero-user-to-paid funnel
was found, so it is not counted as distribution.

## Transferable model

Superhuman's most useful sequence for Tecotype is:

```text
Observe a real high-frequency workflow
→ help a small invited cohort complete it
→ record where guidance is needed
→ encode that guidance into the product
→ release one accurate promise
→ attach referral or reviewer amplification only after return behavior is real
```

The sequence is transferable. Superhuman's performance/status tone,
artificial scarcity, scale of manual onboarding, and one-year startup subsidy
are not.

## Experiments

### 1. Problem-and-workflow interviews

**Stage:** Now; no public client distribution.

**Hypothesis:** One of Tecotype's implemented foundations—calm keyboard
triage, Gmail-plus-IMAP consolidation, or recovering mail from partial or
cross-language memory—solves a frequent, costly job for a narrow professional
segment.

**Execution:** Conduct 8–10 interviews using recent real incidents. Ask about
frequency, current workaround, failure cost, switching history, provider mix,
and what “under control” feels like. Show only clearly labeled development
material where useful.

**Measure:** Count independent repetitions of the same job, recent attempts to
solve it, provider/platform fit, and willingness to schedule a later guided
release session.

**Continue if:** At least five people independently describe the same frequent
job and at least three recently tried to solve it. **Stop or narrow if:** needs
are aspirational, rare, or depend on functionality not implemented.

**Ethic:** Do not imply access, a launch date, or shipped MCP/agent behavior.

### 2. Calm-versus-performance message test

**Stage:** Now; research or notification page only.

**Hypothesis:** A calm-control promise attracts better-fit prospects than
Superhuman-style speed/status language.

**Execution:** Test two truthful messages with the same audience and no
download CTA:

- “Move through email with calm keyboard control.”
- “Find and finish important email without inbox pressure.”

Use the same evidence, development-state label, and `Notify me` or research-
conversation action. Do not test an unsupported quantified time-saving claim.

**Measure:** Qualified conversation requests or confirmed notification intent
per unique session; downstream interview fit matters more than click-through.

**Continue if:** One message produces at least five qualified conversations
and clearer job language. **Stop if:** the signal is mostly curiosity or the
copy outruns the product.

**Ethic:** Minimize collection, disclose purpose, and avoid urgency or fake
scarcity.

### 3. Founder-guided Mac activation sessions

**Stage:** Only after the Mac release gate is met for an invited, safe cohort.

**Hypothesis:** A 20–30 minute founder-guided workflow materially increases
first value and day-seven return while revealing onboarding gaps.

**Execution:** Invite a small source-tagged Mac cohort. Let participants choose
guided or self-serve setup; record selection bias. Guide the selected job, not
an exhaustive tour. Do not use humans to conceal critical missing features.

**Measure:** Account connected, first sync complete, selected first-value
action, voluntary day-one return, voluntary day-seven return, support minutes,
and privacy/trust objections.

**Continue if:** At least five non-team users reach first value, at least three
return on day seven, and guidance themes repeat. **Stop if:** success depends
on manual repair, unsafe send behavior, or promises outside the released
product.

**Ethic:** Obtain consent before observing inbox content; default to screen
areas and synthetic demonstrations that avoid exposing messages.

### 4. Productize one Mac onboarding workflow

**Stage:** After several guided sessions identify repeatable friction.

**Hypothesis:** A short, interactive, skippable-safe workflow can teach the
minimum keyboard actions without Superhuman's forceful pressure.

**Execution:** Encode only the steps repeatedly needed to complete the chosen
job. Use synthetic mail or a safe practice area before touching real messages.
Offer “Not now” where deferral is safe, keep defaults reversible, and explain
local/provider data paths.

**Measure:** Step completion, time to first value, error/undo rate, first-session
abandonment, day-one/day-seven return, and accessibility feedback.

**Continue if:** First-value completion improves without higher error,
abandonment, or support. **Stop if:** completion rises only because the path
traps people, or if users act on real mail before understanding consequences.

**Ethic:** Never copy an unskippable “Inbox Zero” action that mass-archives
mail. Calm control requires agency and reversibility.

### 5. One source-tagged founder invitation

**Stage:** After Mac signing/notarization, website, Gmail audit, payment,
updater, and safe compose/send are complete.

**Hypothesis:** A transparent founder post in the community closest to the
validated job can produce a small cohort of retained users without a broad
launch.

**Execution:** Publish one affiliation-disclosed invitation with accurate
Mac-Apple-Silicon scope, supported providers, price, data path, limitations,
and one job demonstration. Give the source a durable identifier carried from
landing through activation.

**Measure:** Qualified sessions → acquisition action → first launch → account
connected → first sync → first value → day-one/day-seven return → paid.

**Continue if:** At least five non-team users reach first value and at least
three return on day seven. **Stop if:** launch attention exposes reliability,
authorization, or trust failures.

**Ethic:** No manufactured waitlist priority, referral queue-jumping, or
undefined “users” claim.

### 6. Small independent-reviewer brief

**Stage:** After the first released Mac cohort can use the product reliably.

**Hypothesis:** A factual, inspectable brief can earn relevant Mac,
productivity, accessibility, or privacy coverage more efficiently than broad
paid promotion.

**Execution:** Send a small number of reviewers a one-sentence category,
shipped-capability matrix, data-flow explanation, keyboard and local-search
demonstrations, known limits, price, and clean install/update instructions.
Offer no editorial control.

**Measure:** Qualified coverage, attributed first-value users, day-seven
return, and corrections requested—not raw mentions.

**Continue if:** Coverage accurately represents the released product and
produces retained evaluation. **Stop if:** the product state forces caveats
that block normal first use.

**Ethic:** Disclose relationship and access terms; do not ask for favorable
language or suppress limitations.

### 7. One organic proof/search page

**Stage:** After the relevant platform/provider release and only around a
validated job.

**Hypothesis:** A precise, dated page may earn and convert a small amount of
organic intent from people already searching for Superhuman's missing unified
inbox.

**Execution:** Do not buy the exact query. Publish a factual
`/superhuman-unified-inbox-alternative` page only if it is inexpensive to
maintain. Cite Superhuman's dated
official tab workaround; show Tecotype's fully actionable All Accounts and
account-specific views; make sender identity visible; name the current free
alternatives; and state provider/platform scope. Do not claim Tecotype is
faster or imply that a roadmap capability is shipped.

**Measure:** Search impressions and qualified sessions diagnostically. The
primary chain is two supported accounts ready → All Accounts opened → useful
action in each account → day-seven return → distinct paid-job use → purchase.

**Continue if:** Organic or independently recommended visitors produce at
least 20 two-account first-value users, eight voluntary day-seven returns, and
three purchases within 30 days of first value. Stop immediately for sender
mistakes, a core-action paywall, free-incumbent preference, maintenance cost
that cannot support sub-USD-50 CAC, or a Superhuman release that closes the
gap.

**Ethic:** Cite sources, date comparisons, and distinguish shipped, optional,
and planned capabilities. Never transmit mailbox identity or content for
attribution.

### 8. Retention-gated referral credit

**Stage:** After Mac paid conversion and renewal instrumentation are stable.

**Hypothesis:** Existing satisfied users can introduce similarly qualified
users without turning advocacy into spam.

**Execution:** Offer a modest service credit only after the invited user
reaches a defined retained-use milestone, rather than immediately on signup.
Keep contact selection and sending entirely user initiated.

**Measure:** Referred first-value rate, day-seven/day-thirty return, paid
conversion, credit cost, complaints, and organic referrals per retained user.

**Continue if:** Referred cohorts retain at least as well as the comparable
direct cohort and complaint rate stays negligible. **Stop if:** referrals
create low-intent signups, pressure users to contact others, or obscure unit
economics.

**Ethic:** No contact scraping, automatic messages, recipient tracking, or
queue priority.

### 9. Separate Windows readiness cohort

**Stage:** Only after Windows packaging, signing, updating, first-run,
provider, payment, and safe-send gates are complete.

**Hypothesis:** The validated job and calm-control message transfer to Windows,
but activation friction and keyboard expectations differ enough to require a
separate cohort.

**Execution:** Re-run the guided/self-serve learning cycle with Windows users.
Use Windows-specific screenshots, Ctrl-based shortcuts, distribution
instructions, and a distinct source tag. Do not route current demand to an
unshipped build.

**Measure:** The same first-value and return chain as Mac, segmented by OS,
installer/update success, provider, and accessibility configuration.

**Continue if:** Windows users independently reach the release-quality
thresholds. **Stop if:** the Mac workflow is being forced onto platform-
specific behavior or distribution is not trustworthy.

**Ethic:** Say “planned” until a tested public Windows release actually ships.

## What to avoid

- Superhuman's 60,000, 180,000, and 275,000 waitlist figures are attention
  signals, not a reason to manufacture scarcity.
- Do not copy four-hours-saved, twice-as-fast, or “most productive” language
  without Tecotype's own reproducible evidence.
- Do not build a broad SEO inventory before one high-intent page connects to
  retained use.
- Do not offer a one-year startup subsidy before activation, retention,
  support burden, and payback are understood.
- Do not add a large attribution stack before a complete funnel exists; use
  privacy-minimal, first-party source continuity.
- Do not implement hidden read/location tracking or any recipient surveillance.
- Do not use mandatory onboarding to override agency, force destructive inbox
  actions, or mask product gaps.
- Do not publicly promote Mac or Windows access until each platform's own
  release gate is complete.
