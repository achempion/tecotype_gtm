# Quartz launch history

Quartz moved from an agency-owned product and private waitlist to a public Mac
beta within a few months. This timeline keeps prelaunch attention, launch
results, and current product-use claims separate.

Dates are based on public artifacts, pages, archives, and posts. A public object
proves that a release existed, not that anyone downloaded or used it.

| Price | None | Free beta | Free beta; future fees possible |
| Trust proof | Local AI and encrypted-storage claims | CASA Tier 2 added | CASA, demo, screenshots, and Product Hunt badge |

The iOS promise disappeared from the public acquisition page. The privacy
policy and terms still refer to macOS and iOS applications, and the June 9 video
description still mentions iOS. No current public iOS download was found.
Current iOS availability is therefore unknown, not safely described as shipped.

## Interpretation rules

- A public binary or release count is not a download or user count.
- A waitlist form and BetaList listing prove a capture path, not a qualified
  lead.
- Founder content is an acquisition activity even when public engagement is
  small; it is not independent community validation.
- Product Hunt points measure attention on Product Hunt, not Quartz activation.
- Directory pages copied from Product Hunt may be downstream syndication rather
  than independent discovery.
- A changed headline or removed platform promise was replaced, not proven to
  have failed.
- “Hundreds” in daily use is a first-party adoption claim with no published
  definition or raw data.
