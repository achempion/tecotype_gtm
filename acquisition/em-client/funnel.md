# eM Client acquisition funnel

Research date: **2026-07-17**

This funnel is reconstructed from public pages, source, store metadata, search
results, and provider estimates. It stops before setup, account creation,
license activation, mailbox connection, or payment. No eM Client artifact was
downloaded or installed, and no form or checkout was used.

## Funnel map

```text
Provider search / category search / review / partner / Postbox / ad / store
→ homepage, provider guide, comparison, migration guide, pricing, or listing
→ free registration, trial route, paid Personal, paid Business, or mobile store
→ desktop setup, license activation, and first launch not inspected
→ account connection, sync, import, and first useful action unknown
→ return, upgrade, renewal, one-time-major upgrade, referral, or churn unknown
```

## Stage-by-stage evidence

| Stage | Public evidence | Friction | What remains unknown |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Awareness | 1,005 sitemap URLs, 5,500 modeled keywords, partners, Postbox, reviews, ads, stores | Much organic intent is provider navigation rather than client choice | Reach by source, frequency, qualified audience |
| Consideration | Homepage, feature pages, comparisons, Trustpilot, release history, pricing, blog | Broad suite and “best/productivity” language can obscure one urgent job | Page-level assisted conversion |
| Evaluation choice | Free tier, 30-day trial, mobile stores, feature matrix | Free license requires registration; trial starts after desktop installation | CTA click-through and install start |
| Registration | Free-license form asks name, email, country, language, and consent | Personal data before desktop evaluation | Form completion and abandonment |
| Setup | Public page exposes Windows and Mac routes | Package and application outside audit | Completion, signing warnings, errors |
| Activation | License key/account required; trial activates after setup | Post-trial desktop app goes offline without valid activation | Activation rate and time |
| Account connection | Broad provider compatibility and import claims | OAuth/IMAP, auth, migration, and sync can fail | Connected-mailbox rate |
| First value | Email, calendar, contacts, tasks, notes, search, AI, encryption, profiles | Many possible “aha” moments dilute one activation definition | Time to useful action |
| Monetization | Subscription or one-time Personal/Business, Lifetime Upgrades, >50-device quote | Device/use restrictions and version rights need comprehension | Checkout, conversion, refunds, margin |

| Expansion/referral | Partner program, business license manager, Postbox cohort, multiple devices | Incentive quality and channel conflict unknown | Referrals, seats, partner cohort economics |

## Entry routes

### Search

DataForSEO modeled substantial organic visibility, but the ten leading pages
were provider guides and contributed 77.9% of domain ETV. The current brand
query returned the homepage first, while four tested broad category queries
returned no eM Client-owned top-ten result.

This likely creates two very different cohorts:

- a high-volume provider-help audience with uncertain client-choice intent;
  and
- a smaller brand/integration/comparison audience closer to evaluation.

They should not be combined when judging conversion.

### Partners

The partner program offers 30%–45% published margins, license operations,
marketing materials, promotions, training, partner listing, leads, and
co-marketing. A partner may deliver a warmer business or service-provider
introduction than generic organic traffic.

No public data shows partner application, activation, close, or renewal.

### Postbox

The 2024 acquisition delivered a recognizable legacy-client audience,
discounted switching, migration guidance, and later product-feature adoption.
This route has unusually strong intent because the incumbent stopped being
sold and developed.

Eligibility and mechanics are public; redemption and retained migration
cohorts are not.

### Paid and earned media

The Google public ad archive returned four verified text creatives, several
current on the research date. Independent and syndicated coverage surrounds
mobile, version 10, and Postbox.

Neither archive nor coverage exposes impression, click, install, or paid
outcome data.

### Mobile stores

The iOS and Android listings provide discovery and evaluation outside the
desktop website. Mobile was publicly free with no license required. Store
reviews can reinforce or weaken desktop trust.

The listings do not show cross-device account linking, desktop activation, or
subscription contribution.

## Evaluation offers

### Free plan

The current [pricing](https://www.emclient.com/pricing) and
[licensing](https://www.emclient.com/licensing) pages describe Free as:

- €0;
- personal, noncommercial use only;
- two email accounts;
- one device;
- valid for the newest major version;
- forum support; and
- registration required.

The feature matrix includes core email, calendar, contacts, tasks, PGP/S/MIME,
themes, chat, templates, signatures, QuickTexts, labels, search, global
folders, meetings, rules, replies, spellcheck, backup, and duplicate removal.

This is a capable evaluation tier, not merely a time-limited viewer. The device,
account, and usage restrictions create clear upgrade boundaries.

### Trial

The site advertises a 30-day trial. The licensing page says:

- it starts after the first desktop installation;
- after 30 days the app becomes offline until a valid Free or Paid license is
  activated; and
- a free license can then keep the newest major version active for eligible
  personal use.

The trial was not started. Therefore no conclusion is made about onboarding,
data retention while offline, reminders, upgrade prompts, or recovery.

### Paid plans

| Plan | Current public price | Entitlement |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Personal subscription | €39.95/year | Eligible individual; up to three devices; new major versions; AI add-on |
| Business subscription | €49.95/year/device | Organizational use; license manager; new majors; AI add-on |
| Personal one-time | €59.95 | Eligible individual; up to three devices; current major version |
| Business one-time | €79.95/device | Organizational use; current major version |
| Lifetime Upgrades | From €70 | Optional future-major entitlement for one-time licenses |

Personal licensing covers freelancers/sole proprietors but not companies or
institutions. Business licensing is per device; organizations above 50
devices can request a quote.

One-time licenses do not expire for their purchased major version. They include
one year of VIP support, but future majors require a new purchase or Lifetime
Upgrades. The company notes that future features carrying third-party costs
may be limited for one-time licenses.

Subscriptions include new major versions. The licensing page says an expired
subscription makes the application offline until another valid license is
applied.

This clarity is valuable, but “offline until valid license” is a high-stakes
trust moment for an email client. The public pages do not explain the complete
read/export/data-access experience in that state.

### Refund and educational/nonprofit offer

The pricing page states:

- a 30-day money-back guarantee; and
- 30% discounts for NGOs, schools, and universities.

These can reduce risk and support institutional acquisition. Public refund,
discount uptake, and retention are not available.

## Registration and data handoff

The [free-license page](https://www.emclient.com/free-license) asks for:

- full name;
- email;
- country;
- language; and
- agreement to terms/privacy.

The form was not submitted. No validation, email sequence, activation key,
conversion prompt, or post-registration data use was inspected.

The [privacy policy](https://www.emclient.com/privacy-policy) says the company
may process information related to free registration, purchases, support,
forums, promotions, hardware configuration identifiers, and a primary email
address for license/server settings. It also describes behavioral logs and
personalized promotional profiling.

The policy says eM Client does not retrieve or access email, calendar, task,
note, contact, or chat content, with an exception for optional mobile push,
which can require email address, encrypted credentials or token, subject, and
sender. Those are first-party statements; the application and network were not
tested.

## Website instrumentation

The rendered page or source exposed Google Tag Manager, Google Analytics,
Microsoft/Bing, LinkedIn, SharpSpring, Trustpilot, OpenWidget, Cloudflare
Insights, and first-party components. A persistent `tracking-id` cookie was set
before interaction.

This could support:

- source and campaign capture;
- page and CTA measurement;
- lead/customer-relationship management;
- advertising audiences;
- trust/review widgets; and
- on-page chat.

What was not established:

- whether one identifier survives into desktop setup;
- whether free-license registration joins to campaign source;
- whether source joins to successful account connection;
- whether marketing and product telemetry share identity;
- whether consent is honored consistently; and
- which events are used for decisions.

## Consent and trust moments

The rendered homepage showed Accept but no visible decline control, while the
policy describes a decline option. Google Tag Manager loaded and a persistent
identifier was set before interaction. Network behavior was not tested, so
essential versus nonessential processing remains undetermined.

Other trust-sensitive moments include:

- “best” and large cumulative audience claims;
- registration before free desktop use;
- account and hardware identifiers;
- optional mobile push processing;
- automated promotional profiling;
- one-time major-version boundaries;
- possible third-party-cost feature limits;
- expired-subscription offline behavior; and
- cross-platform privacy disclosures that do not align simply.

None proves harm. Each requires precise copy and product behavior because an
email client operates near sensitive personal and work data.

## Funnel strengths

- Multiple qualified entry routes exist: partner, migration, brand, store,
  business, and provider/integration search.
- Free, trial, subscription, and one-time options cover different buying
  preferences.
- Personal versus organizational use and device rights are public.
- A 30-day guarantee reduces paid-evaluation risk.
- Release history and current mobile/desktop listings reduce abandonment risk
  from a visibly stale product.
- Partner and Postbox routes pair audience access with operational support.

## Funnel risks

- Provider-help traffic may inflate the top of funnel without qualified demand.
- A broad feature suite makes first-value definition ambiguous.
- Registration is required for the free desktop plan.
- Trial and subscription expiration can make the desktop app offline.
- Different personal/business/device/version rules create comprehension cost.
- Consent and privacy disclosures need reconciliation.
- Public instrumentation does not show activation continuity.
- There is no source-level conversion, retention, refund, or margin evidence.

## A measurement model for Tecotype

For Tecotype, the funnel should optimize calm, control, and voluntary progress:

```text
qualified source
→ honest job-close page
→ released-platform CTA
→ signed setup/start
→ explicit privacy explanation
→ Gmail/IMAP connection
→ successful sync
→ first keyboard or optional on-device Better-search value
→ voluntary day-seven return
→ €149/year or €15/month purchase
```

Measure at minimum:

- source and message;
- released platform;
- CTA intent;
- setup/start completion;
- privacy-step abandonment;
- account connection;
- sync success and time;
- first useful action and time to it;
- day-one and day-seven voluntary return;
- paid conversion;
- refund/cancellation;
- support burden; and
- source-level contribution.

Do not instrument message contents or promise privacy behavior that has not
been verified. Do not describe the current signed Apple Silicon target as a
public Mac release, invite Windows installation before signed distribution
ships, or present optional on-device Better search and future MCP/agent work as
universally available product behavior.
