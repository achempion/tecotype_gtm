# eM Client product and acquisition history

Research date: **2026-07-17**

This timeline reconstructs public milestones from eM Client, Postbox, release
history, stores, press, and current pages. First-party audience claims are
reported as claims, not independently verified active-user counts.

No historical or current client artifact was obtained.

## Timeline

### 2006–2007: company formation and first desktop release

eM Client says development began in **2006**. Its public
[release history](https://www.emclient.com/release-history?os=win) begins with
version 1.0 entries in late **2007**.

This gives the product a long desktop history and a large archive of version
notes, support threads, provider documentation, and reviews. It does not reveal
the original launch cohort, traffic, conversion, or retention.

### 2008–2017: Windows product, free/paid packaging, and feature expansion

The early product grew as a Windows email, calendar, contact, task, and chat
client. Public release history and old coverage show repeated provider,
calendar, encryption, synchronization, and interface work.

The free-versus-paid model and major-version upgrades became durable
commercial patterns. Older CNET and independent coverage also created a
long-lived web footprint that still appears in content and backlink indexes.

Public evidence for this period is much better at showing shipping activity
than acquisition economics.

### 2018–2019: Mac expansion

eM Client has described launching its macOS version in **2018**, initially with
“teething troubles.” Public Mac release history and later coverage support a
transition from beta/early Mac availability into a maintained desktop product
around 2018–2019.

The platform expansion changed the acquisition category from a Windows Outlook
alternative into a Windows-and-Mac client. Older articles can retain stale
platform descriptions, so current release history matters more than old review
copy.

### June 2020: eM Client 8

[eM Client 8 launched](https://www.emclient.com/blog/new-em-client-8-is-finally-here-388)
on **2020-06-22** with a substantial redesign and new features.

The company's
[version 8 FAQ](https://www.emclient.com/blog/things-you-ask-about-em-client-8-396)
explained that owners without the relevant upgrade entitlement had to purchase
the new major version or revert; otherwise the newer app could remain offline.

Acquisition implications:

- major releases create press, search, email, and upgrade moments;
- existing users become an upgrade audience;
- version boundaries can finance continued development; and
- entitlement surprises can damage trust if not explained before upgrade.

### February 2023: mobile beta

The company invited users to
[test the iOS and Android beta](https://www.emclient.com/blog/come-test-the-beta-version-of-em-client-for-ios-and-android-566)
on **2023-02-16**.

An existing desktop base gave eM Client a natural beta-recruitment channel.
The beta also broadened its store, review, and cross-device acquisition
surfaces before the full launch.

### March 2024: public mobile launch

eM Client announced that its iOS and Android apps were
[officially available](https://forum.emclient.com/t/em-client-for-ios-and-android-is-officially-here/96045)
on **2024-03-22**. Press-release copies appeared across Yahoo Finance,
Silicon UK, AI Journal, and other syndication surfaces.

The launch created:

- Apple App Store and Google Play discovery;
- mobile review inventory;
- cross-platform proof for desktop prospects;
- reactivation of existing desktop users; and
- a new reason for press and partner outreach.

Public material does not disclose launch-day installs, desktop cross-sell, or
mobile retention.

### July 2024: eM Client 10 and licensing expansion

[eM Client 10 was released](https://www.emclient.com/blog/em-client-10-has-been-released-687)
on **2024-07-03**. The release added new features including AI-related
capabilities and coincided with a clearer separation among:

- Personal and Business;
- subscription and one-time purchase;
- current-major rights and Lifetime Upgrades; and
- existing Pro entitlements.

Existing Lifetime Upgrades customers retained eligibility for future major
versions. Existing Pro licensing continued under described terms.

The major release became another syndicated press moment. Syndication increases
discoverability, but duplicate copies should not be counted as independent
endorsements or customers.

### October 2024: Postbox acquisition

[Postbox announced](https://www.postbox-inc.com/blog/entry/postbox-acquired-by-em-client)
the acquisition by eM Client on **2024-10-22**.

The transition included:

- an end to Postbox sales and development;
- support through December 22, 2024;
- Help Center availability through December 22, 2025;
- 100% discounts for recent purchasers on an eligible eM Client one-time
  purchase or first subscription year;
- 50% discounts for older customers on an eligible purchase; and
- exclusions for Lifetime Upgrades and one-time AI add-ins.

An eM Client
[acquisition release](https://via.ritzau.dk/pressemeddelelse/14096605/em-client-acquires-postbox-inc-expanding-its-reach-to-a-broader-user-base?lang=en&publisherId=13562157)
described eM Client as having 2.5 million users and 100,000 companies at the
time. Those figures are dated first-party PR claims.

The acquisition generated independent coverage from WebProNews, TechRadar,
FileHippo, and others. More importantly, it provided a direct switching
audience and a reason to build migration support.

### 2025: Postbox integration and richer mobile product

eM Client 10.3 introduced several Postbox-influenced concepts. The
[Postbox-user guide](https://www.emclient.com/blog/em-client-guide-for-postbox-users-728)
mapped features, shortcuts, account groups, profiles, and migration paths,
while noting that some desired behavior would arrive later.

On **2025-09-29**, the company announced a larger mobile experience with AI,
calendar, contacts, tasks, and notes. This moved mobile from companion email
toward the broader eM Client suite.

The combination of importer, migration content, adopted features, and an
expanding cross-platform product is evidence of an integrated lifecycle
strategy. It still does not reveal Postbox conversion or mobile retention.

### 2026: active releases, content, and version 11 previews

The public release history showed:

- Windows 10.4.5606 on June 30, 2026;
- Mac 10.4.5600 on June 23, 2026; and
- mobile 10.5.308 in early July 2026.

The exact version line can change after this report.

The [blog](https://www.emclient.com/blog) remained active in 2026 with current
how-to material and version 11 previews, including offline mode, pinning, and
Spotlight-related work. Publishing upcoming features can reactivate existing
users and capture search demand, but planned version 11 behavior should not be
described as shipped before release.

The current homepage says the product is trusted by more than 100,000
businesses and four million users. This is higher than the 2024 release's
2.5-million-user figure, but the date, activity window, and unit definitions
are not supplied.

## Audience-claim history

Public eM Client material has used several units:

| Surface or period | Published unit | Interpretation limit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Older company material | More than 500,000 Pro users; more than four million PC installations; more than 1.5 million active | Definitions and measurement period absent |
| October 2024 acquisition release | 2.5 million users; 100,000 companies | First-party PR snapshot |
| Current 2026 homepage | Four million users; more than 100,000 businesses | Current claim; active/paid definitions absent |

These figures may all be possible if they refer to different dates and units.
They cannot be used to calculate reliable growth, paid conversion, or retention
without definitions.

## Commercial-model continuity

Across major versions, eM Client has repeatedly used:

- a registered free personal tier;
- a time-limited full trial;
- paid Personal/Pro and Business use;
- per-device organizational licensing;
- subscriptions;
- one-time licenses tied to a major version;
- paid future-major entitlement through Lifetime Upgrades; and
- support and upgrade benefits as segmentation.

This creates multiple entry and monetization paths. It also makes entitlement
copy a core acquisition asset: version rights, device counts, permitted use,
support duration, and offline behavior need to be understood before purchase
or upgrade.

## What the history supports

- eM Client is not a launch-only product; it has nearly two decades of public
  desktop release history.
- Windows and Mac are current, maintained eM Client platforms.
- Mobile distribution is current and actively updated.
- Major versions repeatedly create acquisition and upgrade moments.
- The Postbox acquisition was paired with a real migration offer and product
  work.
- Content, partners, stores, ads, and provider SEO compound over time.

## What the history does not support

- It does not reveal which milestone created durable active users.
- Release frequency does not equal satisfaction or retention.
- Installations, users, paid users, active users, and businesses are not
  interchangeable.
- Press-release copies do not equal independent validation.
- An acquired legacy base does not prove successful migration.
- Current v11 previews do not establish shipped functionality.
- Current public ads do not establish profitable growth.

## Implications for Tecotype

Tecotype should use history as evidence, not theatre:

- publish a precise release history only after a build is publicly available;
- treat major versions and migrations as trust-sensitive lifecycle work;
- keep update and access rights legible at the current €149/year or €15/month
  price;
- record a public Mac release only after it ships, while keeping planned
  Windows work and future intelligence separate;
- never convert cumulative downloads into “users” without an active-use
  definition; and
- pair any switching campaign with verified import/compatibility behavior and
  support ownership.

As of this research, Tecotype has a signed/notarized Apple Silicon Mac release
path but no verified public Mac release; Windows signed NSIS/self-hosted/store
distribution remains planned. That truth must gate acquisition experiments,
regardless of how mature eM Client's current cross-platform history appears.
