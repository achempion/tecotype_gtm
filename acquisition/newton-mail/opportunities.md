# Newton-informed marketing and distribution opportunities for Tecotype

This document assumes the Tecotype client works and product delivery is handled
elsewhere. It recommends only positioning, public proof, acquisition,
conversion communication, channel design, and attribution work.

Newton shows that brand love can survive multiple closures, but trust damage
becomes a permanent acquisition tax. Tecotype has the advantage of starting
without that debt.

## 1. Lead with one demonstrable job

Newton's current homepage is a broad mature-client inventory. Its 2026 article
is stronger because it gives the reader one emotional idea: the calm inbox
returns.

Tecotype can be more specific:

> Find the email you only half remember—even when you remember it in another
> language.

The Copenhagen-ticket example turns that promise into proof:

```text
“train ticket to copenhagen”
→ “Din togbillet til København”
```

Use calm, privacy, keyboard control, and multi-account support as reasons to
trust and prefer the demonstrated outcome. Do not lead with generic “AI email”
or a long feature grid.

### Test

Create two source-tagged message variants:

1. retrieval-first: `Find the email you only half remember`;
2. control-first: `A calm, keyboard-first inbox for all your desktop mail`.

Use the same exact demonstration and price. Compare qualified page engagement,
evaluation handoff, downstream first-job completion, paid conversion, and
retained payment. Do not optimize to clicks alone.

## 2. Make calm an outcome, not a keyword category

The checked `calm email client` result page was dominated by the Calm
meditation brand. Newton did not rank. The phrase can carry emotional meaning
without providing efficient discovery.

Pair calm with commercial or job language:

- private desktop email search;
- find an old email from partial memory;
- multilingual email search;
- Gmail and IMAP desktop client;
- keyboard-first email client; or
- a relevant, factual competitor-alternative decision.

Select the first page from observed query intent and source-to-paid evidence,
not from volume alone.

## 3. Build a public truth system

Newton's homepage, relaunch article, stores, footer, and changelog do not
describe one current state. Tecotype marketing should maintain one source of
truth for:

- platforms and system requirements;
- providers;
- current capabilities versus planned work;
- price, trial, renewal, cancellation, and refund terms;
- privacy and data flows;
- version and change history;
- support route;
- continuity and data-export terms; and
- the primary CTA for each availability state.

Every public page, store, reviewer brief, partner listing, comparison, and
announcement should draw from that state. This is marketing operations, not a
product gate.

### Measurement

Track stale-surface incidents, broken high-value links, conflicting claims, and
support questions plausibly associated with public copy. Treat reductions as
conversion work.

## 4. Create reviewer-usable proof

Newton's most valuable independent links date from moments with a clear story:
shutdown, acquisition, return, or ownership transfer. Tecotype needs a story
that does not depend on drama.

Prepare a permanent reviewer brief containing:

- the one-sentence retrieval promise;
- the exact Copenhagen-ticket demonstration;
- intended professional user and non-user;
- current provider and platform matrix;
- local-data boundary in plain language;
- price and entitlement;
- screenshots or short factual video;
- a dated change history; and
- a source-tagged evaluation link.

Pitch a small set of email, privacy, productivity, and platform-specific
reviewers whose audiences match the job, limiting platform outreach to public
release routes that are live and verified at campaign time. Count a source
only when the downstream systems return first-job and paid events.

## 5. Own one high-intent decision page

Newton's shutdown created `newton mail alternative`, which competitors still
own. The transferable tactic is not to manufacture fear. It is to answer a
real switching decision permanently and accurately.

Choose one comparison where Tecotype is genuinely relevant. The page should
state:

- who should choose either product;
- provider and platform boundaries;
- the retrieval and privacy differences;
- current price and entitlement;
- migration/evaluation expectations supplied by the product team; and
- a dated verification record.

Avoid a large template-generated comparison inventory. One page with
attributable paid intent is more valuable than dozens of unqualified rankings.

## 6. Use community and referral routes only with attribution

Newton used cancellation discounts, friend discounts, and free months to
reactivate a known community. Tecotype begins without a comparable dormant
base, so an early referral program would lack the same trigger.

After a channel produces retained paid customers, test a voluntary reciprocal
credit:

```text
customer shares a source-tagged link
→ referred person evaluates
→ working client reports first retrieval job
→ billing reports paid and retained milestone
→ both receive a calm, explicit credit
```

Do not use contact scraping, automatic invitations, mandatory sent-mail
branding, recipient tracking, or false scarcity.

## 7. Repair and preserve distribution equity

More than half of Newton's indexed backlinks were reported broken. Tecotype
should prevent the same decay:

- permanent canonical URLs for product, pricing, privacy, changelog, and
  comparisons;
- redirects when names or structures change;
- quarterly checks of linked reviewer and partner routes;
- dated corrections to outdated claims;
- archive pages rather than silent deletion; and
- campaign links that retain source identity without mailbox content.

This work compounds independent coverage and keeps old recommendations safe.

## 8. Do not infer a price lesson

Newton has used roughly $50 and $100 annual offers across different owners and
periods. More than 40,000 paid users did not prevent the 2018 shutdown, and no
public source exposes normalized retention or cost.

Keep Tecotype's decided **€149/year or €15/month** position. Test whether the
specific professional retrieval and control outcome earns that price. A
lower-priced historical competitor is not evidence for a lower Tecotype
offer.

## Recommended sequence

1. Synchronize the public truth system and reviewer brief.
2. Run the two message variants with the same demonstration and price.
3. Seed a small number of high-fit independent reviewers and communities.
4. Publish one high-intent job or comparison page.
5. Reallocate effort only from source-to-first-job-to-paid evidence.
6. Add a referral or paid-media test only after the attribution chain works.

The client team supplies coarse first-job and return events; billing supplies
payment and retention events. Marketing owns the source, message, public
surface, channel, and decision rule.
