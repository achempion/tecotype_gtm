## The legacy Zero launch bundle

### Product Hunt

[Product Hunt](https://www.producthunt.com/posts/zero-8) reports:

- launch date May 27, 2025;
- 850 points;
- #1 product of the day;
- #2 product of the week;
- approximately 1,100 followers in the captured page; and
- a “20% off Zero Pro” launch offer.

This is the clearest high-attention public launch surface. Comments also asked
about Gmail support, rules, native desktop and phone apps, transparency, and
shortcuts. Those are evaluation signals, not evidence that voters activated
or paid.

### Y Combinator

The [Launch YC page](https://www.ycombinator.com/launches/NTI-zero-ai-native-email)
describes the public beta and links directly to `0.email`. The current
[company page](https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/orchid-ai) preserves the
Zero description while naming the company Orchid.

YC supplies durable authority, a launch page, a company directory result, and
secondary social coverage. It does not expose source-level conversions.

### Hacker News

The May 1, 2025
[Show HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=43862892) received 11 points
and one comment. The sole visible response expressed interest in replacing
Superhuman but wanted stronger keyboard navigation.

The post remains a permanent indexed discovery path, but visible engagement
was weak compared with Product Hunt and GitHub.

### Reddit

A May 31, 2025
[self-hosting discussion](https://www.reddit.com/r/selfhosted/comments/1l01joh/)
received 85 votes in the indexed snapshot. The discussion included interest
in self-hosting and substantial skepticism about privacy claims, provider
dependencies, AI handling, and the meaning of the star count.

This is useful discovery and objection research. It does not reveal which
participant visited, connected a mailbox, or remained.

## GitHub as a discovery surface

The public [repository](https://github.com/Mail-0/Zero) had:

| Metric | Result |
| --- | ---: |
| Stars | 10,688 |
| Forks | 1,314 |
| Watchers/subscribers | 40 |
| Open issues and pull requests reported by API | 17 combined |
| Contributors returned by first API page | 100 |
| Repository created | 2025-02-02 |
| Last pushed | 2026-05-26 |
| License | MIT |

The repository itself is permanent discovery, documentation, trust evidence,
and a contribution path. It also links Discord. However:

- stars are not unique users;
- forks may be experiments or abandoned;
- contributors include bots;
- self-hosted usage is unobservable;
- the current Orchid product is broader than the published Zero email
  repository; and
- no public event connects a repository action to hosted activation or
  payment.

The current `0.email` transition page still displays and links the repository.
The current `orchid.ai` homepage does not make open source part of its visible
core message. The inherited star count must therefore be labeled as **Zero
repository attention**, not current Orchid adoption.

## Backlink sample quality

Credible or strategically relevant `0.email` sample links included:

- [Product Hunt](https://www.producthunt.com/posts/zero-8);
- [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=43862892);
- [LogRocket](https://blog.logrocket.com/ux-design/designing-ai-search-right/);
- [Cloudflare's Workers Launchpad](https://blog.cloudflare.com/workers-launchpad-006/);
- independent email coverage; and
- open-source and software directories.

Many sampled rows were multilingual AI directories, scraped catalogues,
expired-domain records, or explicit link-spam networks. Parameterized links
such as `utm_source=tinystartups.com`, `ref=producthunt`, and directory-specific
UTMs prove tracking intent, not traffic.

The `orchid.ai` sample included YC, a Convex launch page, design galleries,
AI-agent directories, personal portfolios, and Tailwind sponsor links. It
also included old-domain detritus and obvious spam. Current-link quantity is
too small and noisy to infer an operating referral engine.

## Current owned and social surfaces

Orchid maintains:

- a dated [blog](https://orchid.ai/blog);
- one public [case study](https://orchid.ai/case-studies/agency-owners);
- X, LinkedIn, and Discord links;
- an Enterprise Typeform;
- a public brand page; and
- campaign pages such as World Cup in messages.

These are potential discovery and evaluation surfaces. No public referral,
affiliate, partner-payout, or user-invite program was observed. The integration
logos and links prove ecosystem positioning, not reciprocal distribution.

## Assessment

The strongest observed pattern is a **launch bundle**:

```text
open-source repository
+ Product Hunt
+ YC
+ founder/community posts
+ permanent branded search results
→ broad awareness
```

The missing half is:

```text
source
→ qualified visit
→ integration connected
→ first useful outcome
→ retained use
→ payment
```

Until that second sequence is public or measured in Tecotype's own funnel,
Orchid is evidence for discovery tactics, not a proven distribution engine.
