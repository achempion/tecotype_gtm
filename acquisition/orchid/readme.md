# Orchid acquisition research

Research date: **2026-07-17**

Scope: **the Zero email-client era at `0.email` and the current Orchid
assistant era, treated as separate funnels**

Market: **Google US-English, desktop where applicable**

## Executive answer

Orchid has inherited a large, visible launch footprint from Zero, its earlier
open-source email client. The strongest observed discovery surfaces are the
[public GitHub repository](https://github.com/Mail-0/Zero), a
[Product Hunt launch](https://www.producthunt.com/posts/zero-8), Y
Combinator's [company](https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/orchid-ai) and
[launch](https://www.ycombinator.com/launches/NTI-zero-ai-native-email)
pages, and public discussions on
[Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=43862892) and
[Reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/selfhosted/comments/1l01joh/).

Those surfaces recorded observable attention:

- the repository had **10,688 stars and 1,314 forks** in the public GitHub API
  snapshot;
- Product Hunt reports **850 points**, #1 product of the day, and #2 product
  of the week for the May 27, 2025 launch;
- the first-party [about page](https://0.email/about) claims more than 15,000
  early-access signups in under three months; and
- the May 1, 2025 Show HN received only **11 points and one comment**.

None of those numbers connects a discovery source to a connected mailbox,
experienced value, retention, or payment. GitHub stars include developers,
spectators, self-hosters, and bots; Product Hunt points are not users; and the
signup claim is first-party, undefined, and unattributed.

The attribution problem became larger when Zero changed category. The current
[`0.email` homepage](https://0.email/) says Zero is now Orchid and offers
three routes: meet Orchid, sign in to legacy Zero, or view GitHub. The current
[Orchid homepage](https://orchid.ai/) positions a personal assistant that
lives in messages, spans inbox, calendar, habits, and personal memory, and
sends every “Get Started” CTA to SMS. The legacy open-source repository and
email-pricing page remain public, but the current Orchid homepage does not
present the same offer or conversion path.

The most relevant conclusion for Tecotype is:

> A public technical surface and a coordinated launch can leave durable
> discovery, but the message, canonical domain, and source-to-paid path must
> remain continuous if that attention is expected to compound.

## Era-separated acquisition paths

```text
open-source or email-tool interest
→ GitHub, Product Hunt, YC, HN, Reddit, or branded search
→ Zero at 0.email
→ free account or seven-day Pro trial
→ mailbox connection and email workflow
→ payment and retention unobservable
```

```text
legacy Zero awareness or current assistant discovery
→ 0.email rebrand page or orchid.ai
→ “Meet Orchid” / “Get Started”
→ SMS to Orchid
→ conversation, integration, activation, and payment unobservable
```

```text
existing Zero user
→ 0.email
→ “Sign in to Zero”
→ legacy hosted client
→ current usage, retention, and revenue unobservable
```

These are distinct paths. A visitor, signup, repository star, or SMS
initiation is not attributable activation-to-paid distribution.

## What appears transferable

- The open-source repository is a permanent public discovery and trust
  surface.
- Product Hunt, YC, and community launches left multiple durable branded
  results around one coordinated release.
- The current Orchid site demonstrates its promise with concrete message
  transcripts rather than a feature inventory.
- A tagged login URL shows at least some source-measurement capability.
- A narrow, memorable category statement is easier to repeat than a generic
  productivity checklist.

## Research boundary

Research used public webpages, server-delivered HTML and headers, public
GitHub and Hacker News API metadata, search extracts, and DataForSEO. The
in-app browser was unavailable, so no rendered-DOM pass was possible; that
limitation is preserved in the detailed files.

No repository was cloned. No source checkout, client, application, installer,
package, binary, release asset, or app payload was downloaded, opened,
mounted, executed, installed, or inspected. No account, Google connection,
mailbox authorization, trial, SMS, Typeform, checkout, or payment flow was
started.

## Implications for Tecotype

- **What appears to be working:** Zero created durable branded discovery through a coordinated open-source, Product Hunt, YC, and community launch; Orchid now demonstrates a broader assistant promise with concrete message examples, but no public source connects either era to paid conversion.
- **What appears weak, discontinued, or inconclusive:** the Zero and Orchid offers are split across brands, domains, CTAs, and categories; current non-brand search is negligible, Show HN engagement was weak, and legacy stars, signups, and links cannot establish current Orchid distribution.
- **What Tecotype should test:** launch one precise desktop-email promise across a public proof page, one high-fit community, and one launch surface, with separate source tags carried through activation, payment, and renewal.
- **What Tecotype should avoid:** broadening into a generic assistant story, treating repository stars or launch votes as customers, or changing the canonical message and domain without preserving a measurable transition path.
- **Evidence strength and remaining unknowns:** public launch, repository, landing-page, search, backlink, and offer evidence is strong for visibility; source-level visits, mailbox connection, first value, retention, subscription conversion, revenue, and the overlap between Zero and Orchid users remain unobservable.
