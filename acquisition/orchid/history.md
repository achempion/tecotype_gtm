# Orchid acquisition history

This history separates the Zero email-client era from the current Orchid
assistant. Dates come from first-party pages and public platform metadata;
causes and commercial performance remain unobservable unless explicitly
stated.

## February 2025: public open-source foundation

The public [GitHub repository](https://github.com/Mail-0/Zero) was created on
February 2, 2025. Its current description frames Zero as an open-source,
privacy-oriented email app with self-hosting, AI, multiple providers, and a
developer contribution path.

The legacy [terms](https://0.email/terms), dated February 13, 2025, describe
an open-source service that can be self-hosted or connected to external email
providers. This established open source and self-hosting as both product
mechanisms and discovery surfaces.

## May 1, 2025: Show HN

The founder's
[Show HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=43862892) presented a public
Gmail beta that prioritized, organized, summarized, drafted, and searched
mail. Public Hacker News API metadata records 11 points and one comment.

The launch remains indexed, but the response was small. The single commenter
wanted keyboard navigation before replacing Superhuman.

## May 2025: privacy, YC, Product Hunt, and public beta

The legacy [privacy policy](https://0.email/privacy), dated May 16, describes
a hosted and self-hosted email product, a free plan, a card-required premium
trial, and subscription billing.

The [Product Hunt launch](https://www.producthunt.com/posts/zero-8) on May 27
reported 850 points, #1 of the day, and #2 of the week. The
[Launch YC page](https://www.ycombinator.com/launches/NTI-zero-ai-native-email)
also announced the public beta.

This coordinated period left the strongest durable branded result page:
official site, GitHub, YC, Product Hunt, Reddit, HN, and independent reviews.
No source-level activation or paid conversion was published.

## Early-access claim

The first-party [about page](https://0.email/about) says more than 15,000
people signed up in under three months. It says the demand favored an
assistant that summarized, composed, and acted.

The page does not date the cohort precisely or define the signup event. The
claim is evidence of captured interest, not proof of use, retention, or
revenue.

## July 18, 2025: first GitHub release

GitHub's public
[release metadata](https://api.github.com/repos/Mail-0/Zero/releases)
shows one release, `v0.1`, published July 18, 2025. It contained no binary
assets in the API response. The repository supplied source availability and a
contribution history, not a measured desktop-client download funnel.

## September 2025 to February 2026: the category broadens

The current Orchid sitemap dates:

- an essay about a new generation of AI applications to September 24, 2025;
  and
- “The End of Inbox Zero” to February 12, 2026.

The latter explicitly reframes email as context for an agent rather than a
destination to process. This is the clearest public bridge from email client
to broader assistant.

Orchid's [terms](https://orchid.ai/terms) and
[privacy policy](https://orchid.ai/privacy), both dated January 16, 2026,
describe a proactive agent spanning email, calendar, chat, documents, CRM,
code, billing, and analytics. The legal entity remains Zero Email, Inc.

## March 15, 2026: Orchid beta

The [beta announcement](https://orchid.ai/blog/orchid-beta-is-here) opened
Orchid to its first 100 users. It moved the promise from faster email to work
being prepared or completed before reaching the user.

The audience also broadened from email-heavy professionals to founders, sales
teams, real-estate agents, operators, agencies, and recruiters.

The 100-user statement defines intended scarcity, not the number who joined,
activated, or paid.

## March to July 2026: owned-content expansion

The [blog](https://orchid.ai/blog) added essays and product updates about:

- executive-assistant economics;
- delegated coordination;
- meeting preparation;
- Granola integration;
- SMS voice notes;
- personal relationships and memory; and
- a World Cup campaign delivered through messages.

The public sitemap contained thirteen dated posts and one agency case study at
the research snapshot. DataForSEO nevertheless modeled only eight ranking
keywords for `orchid.ai`, with the homepage responsible for nearly all ETV.
The content system is visible; search-led acquisition is not yet evidenced.

## Current state on July 17, 2026

The current [`0.email` page](https://0.email/) announces that Zero is now
Orchid while preserving a Zero sign-in and the old GitHub repository. The
current [Orchid homepage](https://orchid.ai/) uses an SMS-first personal
assistant offer.

The Zero repository remained public and unarchived, with its last push dated
May 26, 2026 in the API snapshot. The current Orchid homepage does not frame
its broader assistant as the same open-source email repository.

## What changed and what cannot be inferred

Observed changes:

- email client to cross-tool personal assistant;
- `0.email` to `orchid.ai` as the primary new-user domain;
- web-account CTA to SMS-first CTA;
- open-source/privacy framing to delegation, memory, and personal care;
- Free/Pro/Enterprise pricing page to no visible current plan amount; and
- launch-heavy discovery to an owned blog and case-study system.

Unobservable:

- whether the pivot improved activation or retention;
- how many Zero users moved to Orchid;
- whether GitHub contributors use the current product;
- why pricing is not publicly rendered;
- which channel produced the claimed signups;
- subscription or enterprise revenue; and
- whether any public channel reaches activation-to-paid economics.
