## Current SERPs

### `zero email client`

Observed organic results included:

1. `0.email`;
2. a self-hosting discussion on Reddit;
3. the Zero GitHub repository;
4. a Y Combinator LinkedIn launch post;
5. the current Orchid YC profile;
6. an independent review;
7. the unrelated Inbox Zero product;
8. the legacy Zero about page; and
9. Product Hunt.

This is a durable branded evaluation page with first-party, open-source,
community, accelerator, and launch surfaces. It is strong discovery for
somebody already seeking Zero. It also mixes the old and current products.

### `orchid personal assistant`

The current homepage ranked first. The remaining observed results included a
YouTube short, unrelated personal-assistant services, AI directories, and
other organizations named Orchid. This confirms brand retrieval but exposes
name ambiguity and limited independent product proof.

## Paid search boundary

The Labs snapshot contained no paid ranking rows. No ad-library request was
made because neither inspected landing page created a concrete paid-search
hypothesis. That means advertising is **untested**, not absent.

## Acquisition interpretation

Search currently performs three jobs:

1. preserving branded discovery for Zero;
2. routing existing users to the legacy login; and
3. beginning to establish the Orchid name.

It does not yet demonstrate a scalable non-brand assistant or email-client
acquisition engine. More importantly, the strongest old result page points to
two different categories and conversion paths.

Tecotype should protect category continuity in its title, description,
canonical URL, demonstration, and source-tagged CTA. Search success should be
measured only when a query can be connected to activation and payment.
