# Marketing opportunities for Tecotype from Notion Mail

These are acquisition experiments, not product commitments. They assume Tecotype can market only functionality and integrations verified in current published builds. Transition messaging should be useful even to a reader who never becomes a customer.

## 1. Publish an accurate transition page

Create a permanent page aimed at `Notion Mail shutdown`, dated and updated through the 2026-09-22 lifecycle.

Lead with:

- the exact announced shutdown date;
- the distinction between "announced" and "already shut down";
- what remains in Gmail;
- what Notion says users should save;
- what does not transfer;
- a direct link to Notion's official guidance;
- the last verification date.

Only after that should the page explain where Tecotype may fit. Avoid countdown fear, invented urgency, or implying affiliation with Notion.

**Measure:** search/referral source → supported-client start → first verified useful action → day-7/30 retention → paid conversion. A ranking alone is not distribution.

## 2. Build a migration-outcome comparison

A concise `stays / save / does not transfer / continues in Notion` matrix answers the real job better than a generic "best alternatives" list.

Add a Tecotype column only for current, verified capabilities:

- supported platform and provider;
- local mailbox-access boundary;
- which exported artifacts can actually be used;
- which Notion-specific views, reminders, snippets, or attachments cannot be reproduced;
- what the user must do manually.

Do not promise direct import or feature parity until the delivery exists and
has been verified on every published platform.

## 3. Test a customization-with-durability message

Notion Mail's launch made custom views easy to understand. A Tecotype page can test the adjacent job: calm, controllable organization without requiring the user to trust a black-box priority order.

The proof should combine:

- a specific workflow;
- user-visible controls;
- a precise privacy statement;
- stable platform/status language;
- an export or continuity explanation where shipped.

Compare it with the transition page on activation and paid conversion, not on clicks alone.

## 4. Earn ethical referral discovery

Offer a genuinely useful transition guide to independent Notion communities, productivity publishers, and migration roundups. Give each placement a stable route or source tag that persists through the economic funnel. Disclose affiliation and update the page if Notion changes its dates or export process.

Stop channels that create high bounce, low activation, or early paid churn. Community anxiety is not a reason to relax qualification standards.

## 5. Keep acquisition pages synchronized with release state

Notion's promotional product page, shutdown help, App Store notice, and stale "soon" promises illustrate the trust cost of lifecycle drift. Tecotype should maintain one public release-status source and use it across:

- campaign pages;
- comparison and migration pages;
- store/package surfaces;
- partner briefs;
- support and privacy pages.

Every future-platform or integration statement should be labeled planned until shipped and verified.

## Avoid

- saying Notion Mail is already shut down before 2026-09-22;
- using the HIPAA transition deadline as fear-based legal marketing;
- claiming the inbox closed solely because of poor quality, retention, or revenue;
- promising import of Notion-specific views, snippets, attachments, or reminders before it is real;
- implying that Notion Mail used Skiff technology or that Skiff users map directly to this migration;
- treating shutdown-query rank, backlinks, guide mentions, starts, or free activations as paid distribution;
- downloading or inspecting competitor clients to create the comparison.

## Decision gate

Run the transition page as a bounded experiment. Continue only if it remains accurate, attracts the intended migration cohort, produces a verified first useful action, retains users, and yields attributable paid conversion without elevated refunds or early churn.
