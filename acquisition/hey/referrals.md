# HEY referrals and earned distribution

Snapshot date: **2026-07-17**

## Backlink overview

DataForSEO's public index reported:

| Metric | Result |
| --- | ---: |
| Backlinks | 92,791 |
| Referring domains | 9,367 |
| Referring main domains | 8,336 |
| Referring pages | 76,391 |
| Nofollow referring pages | 9,989 |
| Domain rank | 444 |

The counts include `world.hey.com`. They measure an email service, a public
publishing platform, founder blogs, and unrelated user posts together. They
must not be interpreted as 9,367 sites recommending the paid email product.

## What the one-per-domain sample shows

The top-100 sample contained several distinct systems.

### Company and founder ecosystem

- [Basecamp](https://basecamp.com/) links to HEY from its footer with an
  explicit product recommendation.
- [37signals](https://37signals.com/) links to HEY from company navigation.
- [Ruby on Rails](https://rubyonrails.org/),
  [Hotwire](https://hotwired.dev/), DHH's site, and other projects in the
  founders' orbit link to HEY or HEY World.

This is durable owned and ecosystem distribution. It gives HEY recurring
exposure to an audience already familiar with the makers. Public link data
does not show clicks or conversion.

### HEY World publication links

Many high-ranked sources link to an individual user's HEY World page, feed, or
article. Examples in the sample included personal sites, newsletters, software
projects, and press articles citing DHH's writing. This creates domain
authority and brand exposure, but its visitor intent is often unrelated to
email purchase.

### Product, review, and directory links

The sample also contained:

- product and productivity directories;
- comparison pages;
- a current HEY Calendar reference from Ink & Switch;
- personal reviews;
- articles about the 2020 App Store dispute; and
- links with explicit source parameters from some directories.

These are plausible discovery paths. A backlink is not evidence of referral
traffic, trial starts, or paid subscriptions.

## Launch and earned media

HEY launched publicly in June 2020 with an unusually complete story:

- [TechCrunch's launch report](https://techcrunch.com/2020/06/16/basecamp-launches-hey-a-hosted-email-service-for-neat-freaks/)
  described the mechanisms, $99 price, no IMAP/POP, no historical import, and
  a reported 50,000-person waitlist.
- A later [TechCrunch interview](https://techcrunch.com/2020/07/06/oh-hey/)
  carried Jason Fried's first-party claim of 125,000 signups in 11 days.
- [Product Hunt](https://www.producthunt.com/products/hey) records the June
  15, 2020 launch as number 1 for the day, week, and month.
- The [Apple vs. HEY page](https://www.hey.com/apple/) preserves a long list
  of coverage about the App Store conflict.

The conflict amplified the launch, but causality is unobservable. It also
carried distribution risk: Apple blocked updates until HEY changed the
non-subscriber experience. Conflict should be treated as an event, not a
repeatable channel playbook.

## Owned audience

The homepage and footer promote a newsletter with a first-party
**150,000-plus** audience. That is an owned distribution surface for product
updates and founder/company ideas. It is not a HEY user count.

The founders also publish frequently through HEY World, the 37signals site,
podcasts, books, and company properties. The referral sample supports the
existence of this network, but no public source reports how many newsletter
readers or existing Basecamp customers became activated HEY users.

## Referrals and partnerships

No current public customer referral credit, affiliate program, or general
paid-partner program surfaced in the reviewed pages and searches. That
negative observation is limited by public discoverability.

HEY for Domains includes multi-user administration and collaboration, which
can produce seat expansion after adoption. Public evidence does not expose
invitation rates, team expansion, or partner-sourced revenue.

## What appears durable

- a recognizable maker with a relevant existing audience;
- a product story specific enough for journalists and reviewers to explain;
- many first-party educational pages;
- company-property cross-promotion;
- persistent branded discovery after the launch burst; and
- user publishing that keeps the domain linked and discussed.

What remains unknown is the downstream value of every channel. Votes, press,
links, and signups do not reveal successful migration, habitual use, payment,
or renewal.
