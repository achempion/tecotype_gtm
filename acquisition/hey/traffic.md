Snapshot date: **2026-07-17**

Market: Google United States, English. DataForSEO Labs supplied modeled
keyword and traffic data; Live Google SERP calls verified two current result
pages. These are estimates and result snapshots, not HEY analytics.

modeled organic ETV. Many other high-volume rows lead to unrelated HEY World
posts. Reporting 136,901.58 ETV as “email-product traffic” would therefore be
misleading.

## Current SERPs

### `hey email service`

HEY was the first organic result and absolute result 2. Wikipedia, the Apple
App Store, a critical Reddit thread, and an independent long-term review also
appeared. This is a healthy branded result page: the official site is easy to
find and independent evaluation is visible.

### `gmail alternative email service`

HEY did not appear in the requested top 10. Results included category
roundups, Reddit, Proton, Tuta, and other provider alternatives. One result
mentioned Shortwave rather than HEY.

The pair supports a narrow conclusion: HEY owns branded retrieval but is not
proven to acquire broadly from this Gmail-alternative query.

## Paid search and advertising

The Labs snapshot had no paid rankings. A Google Ads Transparency advertiser
lookup for `hey email service` and a domain creative search for `hey.com`
returned task-level `40102` no-result statuses in the requested US coverage.

This means the requested archive calls did not surface an ad. It does not prove
that HEY has never advertised, that another advertiser identity was not used,
or that advertising outside this market and archive coverage is absent.

## Acquisition interpretation

Search is a supporting channel, not the best evidenced origin of HEY's launch:

- branded retrieval is strong;
- the memorable domain creates generic-query ambiguity;
- HEY World creates a very large unrelated content surface;
- broad category visibility is not established by the checked query; and
- no paid-search evidence appeared.

The more defensible acquisition thesis is founder/company distribution plus
earned launch coverage, followed by branded search. Search demand and HEY World
authority may reinforce that system, but the public data cannot assign
signups to either source.
