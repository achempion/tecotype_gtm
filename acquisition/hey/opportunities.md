# HEY-informed opportunities for Tecotype

HEY proves that email can support a distinct worldview. It does not prove that
Tecotype should imitate its tone, become an email provider, or launch through
controversy.

## Transferable lessons

### Make the product thesis concrete

HEY's story is easy to repeat because it has mechanisms: Screener, Imbox,
Feed, Paper Trail, and Reply Later.

Tecotype should demonstrate one shipped job with the same concreteness:

- find a real message from partial or cross-language memory;
- move through a real inbox by keyboard without gamification; or
- work across Gmail and IMAP in one predictable local-first client.

The audience interviews should determine which job earns the lead.

### Teach unfamiliar behavior before asking

HEY uses a 37-minute walkthrough and many focused pages because its workflow
requires learning. Tecotype can use a shorter, task-complete demonstration:

```text
remembered phrase
→ search
→ matching message in another language
→ open and act
```

The demonstration must use current release behavior and state the optional
on-device model cost precisely.

### Remove trial-payment pressure

HEY's no-card trial means exploration does not become an accidental charge.
Tecotype should preserve the same emotional contract in any invitation:
clear duration, clear price, no hidden renewal, and an easy “Later.”

## Staged plan

### Now: validate one switch-worthy job

Run 8–10 problem interviews with email-heavy professionals who use:

- several Gmail or Workspace accounts;
- Gmail plus IMAP;
- keyboard-oriented Mac workflows; or
- partial and cross-language memory to recover messages.

Proceed only when repeated recent incidents and workarounds support one lead
job. Do not recruit by promising access to an unfinished release.

### Before public acquisition: complete the Mac gate

Tecotype is under active development. A signed Apple Silicon target and release
tooling exist, but a public Mac release is not yet proven. Before sending
reviewers or a public audience to a build, complete:

- dependable no-account and first-run behavior;
- signed and notarized Mac distribution;
- Gmail restricted-API readiness;
- safe compose and sending;
- licensing, payment, cancellation, and recovery;
- release-accurate privacy and data-flow documentation;
- a public website; and
- source-to-activation measurement that does not inspect mail content.

### First Mac experiment: one source-tagged demonstration

After the gate, invite a small, relevant cohort through one page:

```text
specific reviewer, founder note, or keyboard-Mac community
→ source-tagged job page
→ complete demonstration
→ signed Mac evaluation
→ connect one real account
→ complete the lead job
→ voluntary day-2 and day-7 return
→ paid decision
```

Primary metrics:

- qualified visit to evaluation;
- successful account connection;
- lead-job completion;
- voluntary return on days 2 and 7;
- paid conversion after evaluation; and
- support, failure, and refund reasons.

Votes, press, and downloads remain diagnostics, not success criteria.

### Windows: prepare separately, do not imply availability

Windows distribution is planned and release-gated, not shipped. Use Mac
learning to prepare Windows x64 / Windows 11 packaging, signing, updates,
credential protection, onboarding, and support. Test Windows copy and channel
fit only after a real signed Windows release exists. Do not reuse “available
everywhere” language before that is true.

## Do not copy

- HEY's combative or superlative register;
- an artificial enemy or manufactured dispute;
- provider-switch claims, because Tecotype connects existing Gmail and IMAP;
- a huge educational surface before one page converts a validated job;
- a waitlist or launch count without downstream cohorts; or
- annual pricing changes inferred from HEY.

Tecotype's decided price remains **€149/year or €15/month**. HEY's $99 provider
price does not reveal Tecotype's willingness to pay, retention, support cost,
or contribution margin.

## Experiment decision

Keep the demonstration/channel combination only if it produces successful
first value and voluntary return in the intended audience. If attention does
not survive account connection or real mailbox use, repair the product and
funnel before adding more launch volume.
