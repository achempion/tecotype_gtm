# Tatem acquisition report

Research date: **2026-07-17**

Scope: **Tatem’s email product, with earlier Tatem products separated out**

Market: **Google US-English, desktop where applicable**

Tatem is primarily a historical case. Its website remains online, but its public changelog ends in September 2025 and the team joined Wander in October 2025. Tatem also reused the same brand, domain, and app URL for an earlier operations/project-management product. Old reviews, partnerships, and backlinks therefore cannot automatically be credited to Tatem Email.

## Executive answer

The strongest public evidence of how people discovered Tatem Email is:

1. **Conversations about incumbent email pain.** People encountered Tatem in Reddit discussions about Superhuman’s price and access, and some explicitly said they joined Tatem’s waitlist.
2. **Curated design sites.** Dedicated Tatem Email pages appeared on sites such as Curated Design and SEESAW, sometimes with tagged destination URLs.
3. **Branded search.** Tatem currently captures `tatem email`, but its residual 2026 search footprint provides little evidence of non-brand category discovery.

Only the community channel has a visible audience action: self-reported waitlist signup. Even there, activation, retention, and payment are unknown. The same discussions contain reports of waiting many months, difficulty getting access, stale release information, and doubts that the product would arrive. Tatem appears to have generated interest before it could reliably satisfy it.

The most defensible lesson is therefore not “copy Tatem’s referral funnel.” It is:

> Join real problem conversations, but do not scale attention ahead of a credible path to value.

Tatem may also be a direct partnership lead for Tecotype. Since its founder and team joined Wander, the practical opportunity is not to buy code or brand. It is to ask the current rights holder to run a small, consented introduction from Tatem’s email-era audience to Tecotype. Ownership and audience quality remain unverified; see [outreach.md](./outreach.md).

## Discovery paths

The public evidence supports several separate paths, not one observed cohort:

```text
Incumbent price or workflow complaint
→ Reddit discussion
→ user recommendation or disclosed founder response
→ email waitlist
→ self-reported signup
→ later access, activation, and retention unknown
```

```text
Polished Tatem Email landing page
→ curated design-site listing
→ tagged or untagged link to tatem.com
→ visits and signups unknown
```

```text
Existing awareness
→ branded query such as “tatem email”
→ homepage
→ Google signup
→ post-authorization behavior unknown
```

Old Wefunder, G2, Capterra, podcast, and project-management links form a fourth path, but they belong to an earlier Tatem product and are not evidence of email-client acquisition.

## What Tatem tried and changed

Tatem’s acquisition surface and offer changed repeatedly:

- It moved from operations/project management to email between May and August 2023.
- The earlier email page used a waitlist, a “save 3 hours” claim, and prominent AI/autopilot positioning.
- Tatem later said it rebuilt the product, infrastructure, ways of working, and website from the ground up.
- It tried high-touch access, planned referral-led expansion, paid workspace/team billing, self-serve early access, and then a free-forever base plan.
- The current page keeps speed, design, split inboxes, keyboard use, and security, but drops the prominent AI-companion framing and sends “Get started” directly to Google signup.

These changes are observable. Their causes and performance are not. See [history.md](./history.md), [landing-page.md](./landing-page.md), and [funnel.md](./funnel.md).

## Implications for Tecotype

- **What appears to be working:** Tatem’s strongest observable email-era discovery path was discussion where people already had a concrete problem. The path exposed awareness and some self-reported waitlist signups, but does not establish which participant caused them. Tatem was also featured in dedicated design-gallery placements, although their user value is unknown.
- **What appears weak, discontinued, or inconclusive:** Current non-brand search visibility is weak. Paid acquisition is unknown, not disproven. AI-led messaging, numeric time-saved claims, high-touch access, paid team billing, and waitlist capture were replaced or removed; public evidence does not say why. The long wait for access visibly damaged trust.
- **What Tecotype should test now:** Run small, disclosed research conversations around specific email problems. Use an honest research or notification CTA, not a public-product promise. Compare messages such as speed, search, privacy, calm, and inbox control as hypotheses.
- **What Tecotype should test after release prerequisites:** Once the public site, onboarding, distribution, source tracking, and a chosen activation event work, test community-to-activation and curated-gallery-to-activation paths. Test non-brand search pages only for claims the shipped product can substantiate.
- **What Tecotype should test as a partnership:** Ask Christopher LaFerla whether Tatem Inc. or Wander owns the email-era communication rights. Propose a small campaign sent by that rights holder, with explicit Tecotype opt-in and compensation based primarily on qualified activation.
- **What Tecotype should avoid:** Do not buy or import a raw contact list, request Tatem users’ Google data, collect demand far ahead of usable access, imply a referral system exists when it is only planned, manufacture endorsements, copy legacy backlinks from another product era, or judge a channel by backlinks and waitlist size alone.
- **Evidence strength and remaining unknowns:** Community interest and frustration are supported by direct public discussions. Landing-page, offer, and product changes are supported by archived pages and Tatem’s changelog. Search and backlink figures are third-party snapshots. No public evidence connects a Tatem source to activation, retention, or payment, or explains why any offer was changed.
