# MonoMail-informed marketing and distribution opportunities

This file applies MonoMail's public acquisition evidence to Tecotype's
marketing and distribution only. It assumes the published Tecotype offer can
support macOS, Windows, and Linux and that product delivery is handled
elsewhere. Campaign copy must still match the platforms and capabilities
actually public at the time. Mobile is out of scope.

## Transferable finding

MonoMail shows that an early project can earn attention before it earns search
visibility when it combines:

```text
one memorable product idea
+ a credible public proof surface
+ a founder who answers real objections
+ a high-fit community
```

It does not show that this attention became retained or paid usage.

## Experiment 1: one community, one job, one proof page

Choose one validated desktop-email pain and one community where that pain is
already discussed. Publish a useful demonstration rather than a generic
launch announcement.

Candidate tests:

- a multilingual Better-search example for people who cannot recall exact
  phrasing;
- a calm keyboard triage sequence for high-volume desktop mail; or
- a local-mailbox architecture explanation for privacy-sensitive users.

Each community gets its own:

- audience-specific opening;
- public proof page;
- source token;
- follow-up comment log; and
- result row.

| Metric | Role |
| --- | --- |
| Qualified visits | Discovery diagnostic |
| Proof-page engagement | Evaluation diagnostic |
| Desktop activation | Product-value diagnostic |
| Payment | Primary distribution outcome |
| Renewal | Channel-quality outcome |

Do not sum votes across communities as if they were one funnel.

## Experiment 2: turn objections into permanent evaluation pages

MonoMail's comments surfaced access and security questions quickly. Tecotype
should answer recurring objections in permanent, indexable pages:

- what remains local;
- what Tecotype infrastructure never receives;
- provider communication;
- platform availability;
- security contact and disclosure route;
- exact account-connection expectations; and
- what “working” means for the demonstrated email job.

Each founder or team response can link to the relevant section with a
source-specific parameter. The goal is not to win a thread; it is to make the
next qualified evaluator's path shorter and measurable.

## Experiment 3: a canonical live proof and changelog surface

MonoMail's landing page fell behind its GitHub release metadata. Tecotype
should keep one canonical public release/evaluation page whose:

- platform availability is current;
- screenshots match the live offer;
- version and changelog are generated from the release record;
- privacy language matches the public architecture;
- CTA remains stable; and
- source survives the desktop handoff.

Run a release audit:

```text
public mention
→ canonical proof page
→ correct current platform offer
→ attributed desktop activation
→ payment
```

Outdated public proof should block a campaign until corrected.

## Experiment 4: community updates as distinct editorial events

Do not repost identical launch copy across many communities. Use a small
cadence:

1. launch one job-specific demonstration;
2. collect objections and questions;
3. publish a permanent answer or outcome update;
4. return only where the update genuinely helps; and
5. compare paid outcomes by source.

This preserves the founder-led authenticity visible in MonoMail while
avoiding low-response duplication.

## Experiment 5: defer broad SEO until query evidence appears

MonoMail had no modeled owned-domain keywords, yet visible community attention
still existed. Tecotype should not respond by building a broad email blog.

Instead:

- record exact phrases used in high-intent community questions;
- map each credible phrase to one proof or job page;
- measure qualified visits, activation, and payment;
- expand only when several independent people express the same need; and
- retain a canonical desktop-email category in metadata and copy.

Search visibility without activation-to-paid remains incomplete
distribution.

## Experiment 6: keep support separate from the product funnel

MonoMail's donation and one-off customized-build routes can support a creator,
but they do not reveal product willingness to pay. Tecotype should keep:

```text
customer subscription
≠ donation
≠ consulting
≠ community sponsorship
```

If partnerships or community sponsorships are tested, give them distinct
budgets, links, and downstream cohorts. Do not combine their revenue with the
desktop-client activation funnel.

## What not to copy

- Android package or closed-test distribution mechanics.
- Identical promotional posts across several communities.
- A download count presented as a user count.
- Hand-maintained version or adoption counters.
- A CTA that sends visitors to an unavailable or differently capable route.
- Test-capacity claims as evidence of demand quality.
- Discord membership, repository stars, or donations as paid-product
  activation.
- Broad category SEO before a specific query demonstrates downstream value.

## Measurement standard

Tecotype distribution should be counted only as:

```text
permanent public discovery
→ attributable qualified visit
→ attributed desktop activation
→ payment
→ retained paid use
```

Community votes, stars, replies, proof-page visits, and downloads are useful
diagnostics. None completes the sequence.
