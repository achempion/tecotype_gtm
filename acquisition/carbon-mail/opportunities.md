# Tecotype experiments from Carbon Mail

Carbon Mail's useful lesson is not a channel formula. Its public evidence
contains two separate signals whose handoff cannot be observed:

```text
specific founder story
→ visible community attention
→ beta access and product use unknown
```

Separately, the founders reported:

```text
unspecified product testers
→ positive demo response
→ return to Gmail the next morning
```

The founders did not attribute those testers to a source or publish a measured
cohort. Their qualitative product report is still enough to make voluntary
next-day use a release gate for Tecotype acquisition.

The experiments below use planning thresholds. They are not Carbon benchmarks
and should be revised once Tecotype has its own baseline.

## Release boundary

Tecotype can currently substantiate:

- Apple Silicon Mac delivery;
- Gmail and secure generic IMAP accounts;
- keyboard navigation and triage;
- local full-text search;
- optional on-device Better search;
- locally stored synced mail and durable local drafts;
- Split Inbox capability, with launch scope still unresolved;
- reminders; and
- new-message drafts and Gmail/SMTP sending.

Current source inspection also found:

- first-run and no-account onboarding remain incomplete;
- Google's Gmail audit remains a release prerequisite;
- licensing and payment are not implemented;
- proper reply sending and threading are not shipped;
- outbound attachments are not shipped;
- Intel Mac, Windows, Linux, web, and mobile versions are not current products;
- generative writing, automatic AI categorization, MCP, and agent automation
  are future directions.

Marketing and experiments must use the narrower boundary. In particular,
“compose and reply workflows” is not safe current copy even though the working
product description uses that phrase.

## Experiment 1: source and message research now

### Hypothesis

Of two concrete shipped workflows, keyboard triage or remembered-message
retrieval will reveal a stronger qualified switching problem.

### Audience

Recruit email-heavy Apple Silicon Mac users with Gmail or secure generic IMAP.
Keep two problem groups separate:

1. keyboard-oriented people who triage mail frequently;
2. people who struggle to recover messages from approximate or cross-language
   memory.

### Source and message

Make six to ten original, useful contributions in buyer-adjacent communities,
founder-owned channels, and direct research outreach. Split placements as
evenly as practical between the two messages, alternate their order, and keep
source type visible. Each placement should be written for that audience rather
than duplicated. A small heterogeneous sample provides directional research,
not a statistically controlled message test.

Test one proof per cohort:

- “Move through mail from the keyboard.”
- “Find mail the way you remember it.”

Follow the demonstration with exact eligibility: Apple Silicon Mac, Gmail or
secure generic IMAP, local search, and optional on-device Better search. Treat
the Copenhagen example as shipped demonstration copy rather than a guaranteed
benchmark until it has an acceptance test.

### CTA

Use a research action:

- “Help us understand this workflow.”
- “Ask to join the invited pilot when access is ready.”

Do not imply open public access, a free trial, or working checkout.

### Measurement

Record:

- source and message;
- supported setup;
- recurring problem;
- present workaround;
- credible reason to switch;
- willingness to test when the release gate is clear.

A conversation, repeat reply, notification request, or email address is
research evidence, not product activation or retention.

### Planning stop rule

After ten substantive placements:

- reshape the message if fewer than three qualified conversations emerge;
- stop a source when unsupported platforms dominate or promotion is unwelcome;
- continue a problem line when at least five people qualify and the same
  switching reason repeats across at least three.

Carbon evidence: the repeated posts had low single-digit visible scores, some
submissions were removed or filtered, and one founder post appeared in the
captured category SERP. Removal reasons, reach, and downstream value are
unknown. Community content can create discovery, but Carbon has not established
duplicated promotion as a scalable channel.

## Experiment 2: private activation and habit pilot

Run this only after representative first-run onboarding works. Gmail
participants should wait for approved access unless a separately approved test
route exists. A generic-IMAP cohort can test onboarding earlier but cannot
validate Gmail acquisition.

### Hypothesis

A qualified user can reach the promised value without founder rescue and
voluntarily return for the next workday's mail session.

### Audience and invitation

Invite ten to fifteen people from the strongest research cohort. Keep provider
and job cohorts visible rather than merging them.

State the preview boundary directly:

```text
Use Tecotype for your first mail pass for five workdays.

Apple Silicon Mac. Gmail or secure generic IMAP. Local search and optional
on-device Better search.
```

Disclose material limitations, including incomplete proper reply behavior,
until they are fixed.

### Activation

The participant must:

1. complete account connection and first sync;
2. complete the source-matched value event within 24 hours:
   - at least three keyboard triage actions; or
   - find and open a real message from remembered clues.

Downloading the optional 318 MB Better-search model is setup, not activation.

### Retention

Primary:

- voluntary day-one return;
- another core mail action without a founder reminder.

Secondary:

- a core action on at least three distinct days during days two through seven.

### Planning stop rule

- Stop immediately for a severe data, sync, authorization, or sending issue.
- Pause acquisition if fewer than 8 of 10 participants activate.
- Investigate if fewer than 5 of 10 return the next day.
- Investigate if fewer than 4 of 10 use a core action on three later days.
- Interview people who revert before adding traffic.

These thresholds are deliberately demanding planning defaults for a tiny
invited cohort, not statistical claims.

Carbon evidence: demo enthusiasm did not prevent next-morning reversion. A
forced redirect was only a proposed workaround. Tecotype should measure chosen
use, not manufacture it.

## Experiment 3: one-source acquisition-to-retention test

Run this only after:

- usable first run;
- approved Gmail access;
- accurate public site and privacy material;
- open distribution;
- source attribution reviewed for privacy;
- severe compose, reply, sync, and sending gaps resolved.

### Hypothesis

A buyer-facing product demonstration can convert a relevant visit into
activated and retained use, not only builder curiosity.

### Design

Use the strongest private-pilot segment and one source per cohort. Start with
one original community or founder-channel demonstration, not a directory batch
or broad launch.

Landing message:

- “Find mail the way you remember it”; or
- “Move through mail from the keyboard.”

Add exact Apple Silicon, provider, and local-processing facts. Do not lead with
“AI.”

CTA:

- “Download for Apple Silicon Mac.”

Do not say free or trial unless that offer is decided and implemented.

### Measurement

Preserve:

```text
attributed visit
→ download
→ first launch
→ account connection
→ first sync
→ source-matched value event
→ day-one return
→ day-seven retained use
```

Use a short-lived, privacy-reviewed source handoff with a documented deletion
or aggregation point. Do not introduce a permanent install identity solely for
marketing attribution.

### Planning stop rule

- Diagnose the funnel if 100 qualified downloads produce fewer than 10
  activations.
- Pause a source if fewer than 6 of its first 20 activated users retain at day
  seven.
- Stop immediately if the placement becomes repetitive promotion.

Carbon evidence: the founder post is more discoverable for a category query
than the product domain, but Carbon exposes no source-to-activation or
source-to-retention evidence.

## Experiment 4: source to paid retention

Run this only after licensing, payment, cancellation, and the offer are
implemented.

### Hypothesis

The strongest retained audience will accept Tecotype's decided professional
price when the value and commitment are stated plainly.

### Offer

Use the winning product promise, then show:

```text
€149 per year
or €15 per month
```

Present one Individual plan with identical features, annual first. Use the
exact released checkout action. “Start trial” is valid only if trial terms are
decided and implemented.

### Measurement

- visit to checkout;
- purchase;
- source-matched product activation;
- week-four core use;
- cancellation and refund rate;
- renewal when the cohort matures.

Checkout alone is not paid activation.

### Planning stop rule

The current traffic model's pessimistic assumption is one purchase per 1,000
relevant visitors. Do not expand when 5,000 relevant visits produce fewer than
five paid activations. Stop when acquisition cost approaches realized gross
cash per customer; the sustainable ceiling is below €149 after VAT, payment
fees, refunds, support, and monthly-plan cash timing.

Carbon evidence: Carbon reports $0 paid spend, promises a free tier, and
publishes no payment evidence. It is not a pricing benchmark.

## What Tecotype should avoid

- Do not launch a public Gmail or download campaign before the release gate.
- Do not count votes, backlinks, rankings, downloads, or OAuth completion as
  success.
- Do not duplicate one promotional post across loosely related communities.
- Do not use a forced redirect, default-client hijack, streak, guilt, or
  inbox-zero pressure to create return behavior.
- Do not copy Carbon's permanent free-tier pledge. Tecotype's price is decided;
  trial and free-plan mechanics are not.
- Do not claim “11 hours back,” “decision fatigue after two hours,” “many
  users,” “zero unread,” “zero tracking,” or “data never leaves your
  hardware.”
- Do not market automatic AI classification, generative writing, MCP, agent
  automation, proper replies, attachments, or unshipped platforms.
- Do not make Split Inbox a guaranteed launch promise until release scope is
  decided.
- Do not infer that Carbon's sparse SEO, directory, or ad evidence proves those
  channels cannot work. It proves only that Carbon has not established them
  publicly.
